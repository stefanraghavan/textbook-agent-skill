| 32. Shaft with two splines

<!-- Description: Cross-sectional diagram of a shaft with two opposite splines, showing radius $r$, spline dimensions $a$ and $b$, and applied moment $M$. --> | $K = 2Cr^4$ where $C$ varies with $\dfrac{b}{r}$ as follows.

For $0 \le \dfrac{b}{r} \le 0.5$:

$$
C = K_1 + K_2\frac{b}{r} + K_3\left(\frac{b}{r}\right)^2 + K_4\left(\frac{b}{r}\right)^3
$$

where for $0.2 \le a/b \le 1.4$,

$$
K_1 = 0.7854
$$

$$
K_2 = 0.0204 - 0.1307\frac{a}{b} + 0.1157\left(\frac{a}{b}\right)^2
$$

$$
K_3 = -0.2075 + 1.1544\frac{a}{b} - 0.5937\left(\frac{a}{b}\right)^2
$$

$$
K_4 = 0.3608 - 2.2582\frac{a}{b} + 3.7336\left(\frac{a}{b}\right)^2
$$ | At $M$, $\tau = \dfrac{TB}{r^3}$ where $B$ varies with $\dfrac{b}{r}$ as follows. For $0 \le \dfrac{b}{r} \le 0.5$,

$$
B = K_1 + K_2\frac{b}{r} + K_3\left(\frac{b}{r}\right)^2 + K_4\left(\frac{b}{r}\right)^3
$$

where for $0.2 \le a/b \le 1.4$,

$$
K_1 = 0.6366
$$

$$
K_2 = 0.0069 - 0.0229\frac{a}{b} + 0.0637\left(\frac{a}{b}\right)^2
$$

$$
K_3 = -0.0675 + 0.3996\frac{a}{b} - 1.0514\left(\frac{a}{b}\right)^2
$$

$$
K_4 = 0.3582 - 1.8324\frac{a}{b} + 1.5393\left(\frac{a}{b}\right)^2
$$
