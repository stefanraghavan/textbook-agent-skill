# EXAMPLE

An annular bimetallic plate has a 3-in outer diameter and a 2.1-in inner diameter; the top portion is 0.020-in-thick stainless steel, and the bottom is 0.030-in-thick titanium (see Fig. 11.4). For the stainless steel $E = 28(10^6)\,\text{lb/in}^2$, $\nu = 0.3$, and $\gamma = 9.6(10^{-6})\,\text{in/in/}^\circ\text{F}$; for the titanium $E = 17(10^6)\,\text{lb/in}^2$, $\nu = 0.3$, and $\gamma = 5.7(10^{-6})\,\text{in/in/}^\circ\text{F}$. The outer edge is simply supported, and the inner edge is elastically supported by a spring which develops 500 lb of load for each inch of deflection. It is necessary to determine the center deflection and the maximum stress for a temperature rise of $50^\circ\text{F}$.

**Solution.** First evaluate the constants $K_{1p}$, $K_{2p}$, and $K_{3p}$, the equivalent stiffness $D_e$, and the equivalent Poisson's ratio $\nu_e$. From Eq. (11.3-6),

$$
K_{1p} = 4 + 6\frac{0.02}{0.03} + 4\left(\frac{2}{3}\right)^2 + \frac{28}{17}\left(\frac{2}{3}\right)^3\left(\frac{1-0.3}{1-0.3}\right) + \frac{17}{28}\left(\frac{3}{2}\right)\left(\frac{1-0.3}{1-0.3}\right)
$$

$$
= 11.177
$$

Since $\nu_a = \nu_b$ for this example, $K_{3p} = K_{2p} = 11.986$ and the equivalent Poisson's ratio $\nu_e = 0.3$. From Eq. (11.3-1),

$$
D_e = \frac{28(10^6)(0.02^3)}{12(1-0.3^2)}(11.986) = 246\,\text{lb-in}
$$

Table 11.2, case 8a, treats an annular plate with the inner edge free and the outer edge simply supported. As in Eq. (11.3-5), the term $\gamma \Delta T/t$ must be replaced by

$$
\frac{6(\gamma_b-\gamma_a)(T-T_o)(t_a+t_b)}{t_b^2K_{1p}} = \frac{6(5.7-9.6)(10^{-6})(50)(0.02+0.03)}{(0.03^2)(11.177)} = -0.00582
$$

Since $b/a = 1.05/1.5 = 0.7$ and $\nu_e = 0.3$, the tabulated data can be used and $K_{yb} = -0.255$ and $K_{\theta b} = 0.700$. Therefore, $y_b = -0.255(-0.00582)(1.5^2) = 0.00334\,\text{in}$ and $\theta_b = 0.7(-0.00582)(1.5) = -0.0061\,\text{rad}$. There are no moments or edge loads in the plate, and so $M_{rb} = 0$, and $Q_b = 0$. Case 1a treats an annular plate with an annular line load. For $r_o = b$ and $b/a = 0.7$, $K_{yb} = -0.1927$ and $K_{\theta b} = 0.6780$. Therefore, $y_b = -0.1927w(1.5^3)/246 = -0.002645w$, $\theta_b = -0.678w(1.5^2)/246 = 0.0062w\,\text{rad}$, $M_{rb} = 0$, and $Q_b = 0$.

Equating the deflection of the inner edge of the plate to the deflection of the elastic support gives $y_b = 0.00334 - 0.002645w = 2\pi(1.05)w/500 = 0.0132w$. Solving for $w$, we obtain $w = 0.211\,\text{lb/in}$ for a total center load of 1.39 lb. The deflection of the inner edge is $y_b = 0.0132(0.211) = 0.00279\,\text{in}$. The maximum moment developed in the plate is the tangential moment at the inner edge:
