The principal stresses are found using Eq. (2.3-20):†

$$
\sigma_p^3 - (4007 + 9996 - 2000)\sigma_p^2 + [(4007)(9996) + (9996)(-2000)
$$

$$
+ (-2000)(4007) - 2794^2 - 0 - 0]\sigma_p - [(4007)(9996)(-2000) + 2(2794)(0)(0)
$$

$$
- (4007)(0^2) - (9996)(0^2) - (-2000)(2794^2)] = 0
$$

or

$$
\sigma_p^3 - 12.003(10^3)\sigma_p^2 + 4.2415(10^6)\sigma_p + 64.495(10^9) = 0
$$

Solving this gives $\sigma_p = 11,100,\ 2906,$ and $-2000$ lb/in.$^2$, which are the principal stresses $\sigma_1$, $\sigma_2$, and $\sigma_3$, respectively. Obviously, the maximum tensile stress is 11,100 lb/in.$^2$. Again, the maximum shear stress comes from Eq. (2.3-25), and is $\frac{1}{2}[11,100 - (-2000)] = 6550$ lb/in.$^2$.

Note that for this problem, if the pipe is a ductile material, and one were looking at failure due to shear stress (see Sec. 3.7), the stress conditions for the pipe are more severe at the inner surface compared with the outer surface.

† *Note:* Since $\tau_{yz} = \tau_{zx} = 0$, $\sigma_z$ is one of the principal stresses and the other two can be found from the plane stress equations. Consequently, the other two principal stresses are in the $xy$ plane.
