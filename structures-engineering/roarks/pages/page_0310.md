## 3. Triangular section, base inward

![Figure 9.1-3: Triangular section, base inward](figures/fig9_1_3.png)
<!-- Description: A triangular cross section with base at the bottom, dimensions labeled b along the base, d vertically on the right, c from the centroidal axis to the inner edge, and radius R from the center of curvature to the inner edge. -->

$$
\frac{e}{c}=\frac{R}{c}-\frac{4.5}{\left(\frac{R}{c}+2\right)\ln\!\left(\frac{R/c+2}{R/c-1}\right)-3}, \qquad c=\frac{d}{3}
$$

$$
k_i=\frac{1}{2e/c}\frac{1-e/c}{R/c-1}
$$

$$
k_o=\frac{1}{4e/c}\frac{2+e/c}{R/c+2}
$$

$$
\int_{\text{area}} \frac{dA}{r}
= b\left[\left(\frac{R}{3c}+\frac{2}{3}\right)\ln\!\left(\frac{R/c+2}{R/c-1}\right)-1\right]
$$

*(Note: $e/c$, $k_i$, and $k_o$ are independent of the width $b$)*

## 4. Triangular section, base outward

![Figure 9.1-4: Triangular section, base outward](figures/fig9_1_4.png)
<!-- Description: An inverted triangular cross section with top width labeled b1, total depth d on the right, c from the centroidal axis to the lower apex region, and radius R from the center of curvature to the top side. -->

$$
\frac{e}{c}=\frac{R}{c}-\frac{1.125}{1.5-\left(\frac{R}{c}-1\right)\ln\!\left(\frac{R/c+0.5}{R/c-1}\right)}, \qquad c=\frac{2d}{3}
$$

$$
k_i=\frac{1}{8e/c}\frac{1-e/c}{R/c-1}
$$

$$
k_o=\frac{1}{4e/c}\frac{2e/c+1}{2R/c+1}
$$

$$
\int_{\text{area}} \frac{dA}{r}
= b_1\left[1-\frac{2}{3}\left(\frac{R}{c}-1\right)\ln\!\left(\frac{R/c+0.5}{R/c-1}\right)\right]
$$

*(Note: $e/c$, $k_i$, and $k_o$ are independent of the width $b_1$)*

## 5. Diamond

![Figure 9.1-5: Diamond](figures/fig9_1_5.png)
<!-- Description: A diamond-shaped cross section with horizontal width b, vertical depth d, centroidal distances c marked above and below the centerline, and radius R from the center of curvature to the left side. -->

$$
\frac{e}{c}=\frac{R}{c}-\frac{1}{\frac{R}{c}\ln\!\left[1-\left(\frac{c}{R}\right)^2\right]+\ln\!\left(\frac{R/c+1}{R/c-1}\right)}
$$

$$
k_i=\frac{1}{6e/c}\frac{1-e/c}{R/c-1}
$$

$$
k_o=\frac{1}{6e/c}\frac{1+e/c}{R/c+1}
$$

$$
\int_{\text{area}} \frac{dA}{r}
= b\left[\frac{R}{c}\ln\!\left[1-\left(\frac{c}{R}\right)^2\right]+\ln\!\left(\frac{R/c+1}{R/c-1}\right)\right]
$$

*(Note: $e/c$, $k_i$, and $k_o$ are independent of the width $b$)*
