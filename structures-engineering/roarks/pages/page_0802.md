800 \[APP. A

$z$  
polar axis through $C$

$x', y'$  
rectangular axes in the plane of the section, with origin at $C$, inclined at a counterclockwise angle $\theta$ from $x, y$

$1,\ 2$  
principal axes at $C$ inclined at a counterclockwise angle $\theta_p$ from $x, y$

$r$  
the distance from $C$ to the $dA$ element, $r = \sqrt{x^2 + y^2}$

By definition,

Moments of inertia:  
$$
I_x = \int_A y^2\, dA,\qquad I_y = \int_A x^2\, dA
$$

Polar moment of inertia:  
$$
I_z = J = \int_A r^2\, dA = I_x + I_y = I_{x'} + I_{y'} = I_1 + I_2
$$

Product of inertia:  
$$
I_{xy} = \int_A xy\, dA
$$

Radii of gyration:  
$$
k_x = \sqrt{I_x/A},\qquad k_y = \sqrt{I_y/A}
$$

Parallel axis theorem:  
$$
I_X = I_x + Ay_c^2,\qquad I_Y = I_y + Ax_c^2,\qquad I_{XY} = I_{xy} + Ax_c y_c
$$

Transformation equations:  
$$
I_{x'} = I_x \cos^2 \theta + I_y \sin^2 \theta - I_{xy}\sin 2\theta
$$
$$
I_{y'} = I_x \sin^2 \theta + I_y \cos^2 \theta + I_{xy}\sin 2\theta
$$
$$
I_{x'y'} = \frac{1}{2}(I_x - I_y)\sin 2\theta + I_{xy}\cos 2\theta
$$

Principal moments of inertia and directions:  
$$
I_{1,2} = \frac{1}{2}\left[(I_x + I_y) \pm \sqrt{(I_y - I_x)^2 + 4I_{xy}^2}\right],\qquad I_{12} = 0,
$$
$$
\theta_p = \frac{1}{2}\tan^{-1}\left(\frac{2I_{xy}}{I_y - I_x}\right)
$$

Upon the determination of the two principal moments of inertia, $I_1$ and $I_2$, two angles, $90^\circ$ apart, can be solved for from the equation for $\theta_p$. It may be obvious which angle corresponds to which principal moment of inertia. If not, one of the angles must be substituted into the equations $I_{x'}$ and $I_{y'}$ which will again yield the principal moments of inertia but also their orientation.

Note, if either one of the $xy$ axes is an axis of symmetry, $I_{xy} = 0$, with $I_x$ and $I_y$ being the principal moments of inertia of the section.
