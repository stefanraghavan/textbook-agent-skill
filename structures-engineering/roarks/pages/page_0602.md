# TABLE 13.1 Formulas for membrane stresses and deformations in thin-walled pressure vessels *(Continued)*

## 3. Spherical

![Figure 13.1: Spherical shell geometry and stress directions](figures/fig13_1.png)
<!-- Description: A spherical shell with radius $R_2$ and thickness $t$, showing principal membrane stresses $\sigma_1$ and $\sigma_2$. Condition $R_2/t > 10$ is indicated. -->

$$
\frac{R_2}{t} > 10
$$

### 3a. Uniform internal or external pressure; $q$ force/unit area; tangential edge support

![Figure 13.2: Spherical segment under uniform pressure](figures/fig13_2.png)
<!-- Description: A spherical segment with radius $R_2$, angle $\theta$, uniform pressure loading, and tangential membrane stress $\sigma_1$. -->

$$
\sigma_1=\sigma_2=\frac{qR_2}{2t}
$$

$$
\Delta R=\frac{qR_2^2(1-\nu)\sin\theta}{2Et}
$$

$$
\Delta R_2=\frac{qR_2^2(1-\nu)}{2Et}
$$

$$
\Delta y=\frac{qR_2^2(1-\nu)(1-\cos\theta)}{2Et}
$$

$$
\psi=0
$$

### 3b. Filled to depth $d$ with liquid of density $\delta$ force/unit volume; tangential edge support

![Figure 13.3: Spherical vessel partially filled with liquid](figures/fig13_3.png)
<!-- Description: A spherical vessel filled to depth $d$, showing liquid surface, radius $R_2$, horizontal radius $R$, angle $\theta$, and vertical coordinate $y$. -->

At any level $y$ below the liquid surface, $y<d$

$$
\sigma_1=\frac{\delta R_2^2}{6t}\left(3\frac{d}{R_2}-1+\frac{2\cos^2\theta}{1+\cos\theta}\right)
$$

$$
\sigma_2=\frac{\delta R_2^2}{6t}\left[3\frac{d}{R_2}-5+\frac{(3+2\cos\theta)2\cos\theta}{1+\cos\theta}\right]
$$

$$
\Delta R=-\frac{\delta R_2^3\sin\theta}{6Et}\left[3(1-\nu)\frac{d}{R_2}-5+\nu+2\cos\theta\frac{3+(2-\nu)\cos\theta}{1+\cos\theta}\right]
$$

$$
\Delta y=\frac{\delta R_2^3}{6Et}\left\{3(1-\nu)\left[\frac{d}{R_2}(1-\cos\theta)+\cos\theta\right]-(2-\nu)\cos^2\theta+(1+\nu)\left(1+2\ln\frac{2}{1+\cos\theta}\right)\right\}
$$

$$
\psi=-\frac{\delta R_2^2}{Et}\sin\theta
$$

Weight of liquid $=P=\delta\pi d^2\left(R_2-\frac{d}{3}\right)$

*(Note: See the note in case 2b regarding the discrepancy in the meridional slope at $y=d$)*

At any level $y$ above the liquid level use case 3d with the load equal to the entire weight of the liquid

### 3c. Own weight, $\delta$ force/unit volume; tangential top edge support

![Figure 13.4: Spherical shell under own weight](figures/fig13_4.png)
<!-- Description: A spherical shell supported tangentially at the top edge and loaded by its own weight, showing stresses, angle $\theta$, radius $R$, and vertical coordinate $y$. -->

$$
\sigma_1=\frac{\delta R_2}{1+\cos\theta}, \qquad \sigma_2=-\delta R_2\left(\frac{1}{1+\cos\theta}-\cos\theta\right)
$$

Max tensile $\sigma_2=\frac{\delta R_2}{2}$ at $\theta=0$

$$
\sigma_2=0 \qquad \text{at } \theta=51.83^\circ
$$

$$
\Delta R=-\frac{\delta R_2^2\sin\theta}{E}\left(\frac{1+\nu}{1+\cos\theta}-\cos\theta\right)
$$

$$
\Delta y=\frac{\delta R_2^2}{E}\left[\sin^2\theta+(1+\nu)\ln\frac{2}{1+\cos\theta}\right]
$$

$$
\psi=-\frac{\delta R_2}{E}(2+\nu)\sin\theta
$$
