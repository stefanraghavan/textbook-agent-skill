# TABLE 13.1 Formulas for membrane stresses and deformations in thin-walled pressure vessels *(Continued)*

| Case | Description | Formulas/Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 1c. | Uniform internal or external pressure, $q$ force/unit area (ends capped) | At points away from the ends  $$\sigma_1=\frac{qR}{2t}$$ $$\sigma_2=\frac{qR}{t}$$ $$\Delta R=\frac{qR^2}{Et}\left(1-\frac{\nu}{2}\right)$$ $$\Delta y=\frac{qRy}{Et}(0.5-\nu)$$ $$\psi=0$$ |
| 1d. | Linearly varying radial pressure, $q$ force/unit area | $$q=\frac{q_0y}{l}$$ (where $y$ must be measured from a free end. If pressure starts away from the end, see case 6 in Table 13.2)  $$\sigma_1=0$$ $$\sigma_2=\frac{qR}{t}=\frac{q_0Ry}{lt}$$ $$\Delta R=\frac{qR^2}{Et}=\frac{q_0R^2y}{Etl}$$ $$\Delta y=-\frac{q_0R\nu y^2}{2Etl}$$ $$\psi=\frac{q_0R^2}{Etl}$$  ![Figure 1d: Linearly varying radial pressure diagram](figures/fig1d.png) <!-- Description: A shell diagram showing linearly varying radial pressure on the sides, with dimensions labeled l and y, and pressure intensity q0 at the top. --> |
| 1e. | Own weight, $\delta$ force/unit volume; top edge support, bottom edge free | $$\sigma_1=\delta y$$ $$\sigma_2=0$$ $$\Delta R=-\frac{\delta \nu R y}{E}$$ $$\Delta y=\frac{\delta y^2}{2E}$$ $$\psi=-\frac{\delta \nu R}{E}$$ |
