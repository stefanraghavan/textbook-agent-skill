From Eq. (2.3-12), the directional cosines for the direction of $\tau$ are

$$
l_\tau = \frac{1}{14.95} \left[ (14 - 19.21)(2/\sqrt{14}) + 7(-1/\sqrt{14}) + (-7)(3/\sqrt{14}) \right] = -0.687
$$

$$
m_\tau = \frac{1}{14.95} \left[ 7(2/\sqrt{14}) + (10 - 19.21)(-1/\sqrt{14}) + (0)(3/\sqrt{14}) \right] = 0.415
$$

$$
n_\tau = \frac{1}{14.95} \left[ (-7)(2/\sqrt{14}) + (0)(-1/\sqrt{14}) + (35 - 19.21)(3/\sqrt{14}) \right] = 0.596
$$

**Plane Stress.** For the state of plane stress shown in Fig. 2.11(a), $\sigma_z = \tau_{yz} = \tau_{zx} = 0$. Plane stress transformations are normally performed in the $xy$ plane, as shown in Fig. 2.11(b). The angles relating the $x'y'z'$ axes to the $xyz$ axes are

$$
\theta_{x'x} = \theta, \qquad \theta_{x'y} = 90^\circ - \theta, \qquad \theta_{x'z} = 90^\circ
$$

$$
\theta_{y'x} = \theta + 90^\circ, \qquad \theta_{y'y} = \theta, \qquad \theta_{y'z} = 90^\circ
$$

$$
\theta_{z'x} = 90^\circ, \qquad \theta_{z'y} = 90^\circ, \qquad \theta_{z'z} = 0
$$

Thus the directional cosines are

$$
l_{x'} = \cos \theta \qquad m_{x'} = \sin \theta \qquad n_{x'} = 0
$$

$$
l_{y'} = -\sin \theta \qquad m_{y'} = \cos \theta \qquad n_{y'} = 0
$$

$$
l_{z'} = 0 \qquad m_{z'} = 0 \qquad n_{z'} = 1
$$

The last rows and columns of the stress matrices are zero so the stress matrices can be written as

$$
[\sigma]_{xy} =
\begin{bmatrix}
\sigma_x & \tau_{xy} \\
\tau_{xy} & \sigma_y
\end{bmatrix}
\qquad (2.3\text{-}13)
$$
