**13. T-beam or channel section**

![Figure 13: T-beam or channel section](figures/fig13.png)
<!-- Description: Two cross-sectional diagrams of a T-beam or channel section with dimensions labeled $b_1$, $\tfrac{1}{2}b_1$, $b$, $t$, $d$, $c$, $c_1$, and radius $R$. -->

$$
\frac{d}{c}=\frac{2[b_1/b+(1-b_1/b)(t/d)]}{b_1/b+(1-b_1/b)(t/d)^2}
\qquad
\frac{c_1}{c}=\frac{d}{c}-1
$$

$$
\frac{e}{c}=\frac{R}{c}-
\frac{(d/c)[b_1/b+(1-b_1/b)(t/d)]}
{
\frac{b_1}{b}\ln\!\left(\frac{d/c+R/c-1}{(d/c)(t/d)+R/c-1}\right)
+\ln\!\left(\frac{(d/c)(t/d)+R/c-1}{R/c-1}\right)
}
$$

$$
k_i=\frac{I_c}{Ac^2(R/c-1)}\frac{1-e/c}{e/c}
$$

where

$$
\frac{I_c}{Ac^2}
=\frac{1}{3}\left(\frac{d}{c}\right)^2
\left[
\frac{b_1/b+(1-b_1/b)(t/d)^3}
{b_1/b+(1-b_1/b)(t/d)}
\right]-1
$$

$$
k_o=\frac{I_c}{Ac^2(e/c)}
\frac{d/c+e/c-1}{R/c+d/c-1}
\frac{1}{d/c-1}
$$

*(Note: While $e/c$, $k_i$, and $k_o$ depend upon the width ratio $b_1/b$, they are independent of the width $b$)*

(When $b_1/b=\tfrac{1}{4}$, $t/d=\tfrac{1}{4}$)

**14. Symmetrical I-beam or hollow rectangular section**

![Figure 14: Symmetrical I-beam or hollow rectangular section](figures/fig14.png)
<!-- Description: Two cross-sectional diagrams of a symmetrical I-beam or hollow rectangular section with dimensions labeled $b_1$, $\tfrac{1}{3}b_1$, $b$, $t$, $d$, $c$, and radius $R$. -->

$$
\frac{e}{c}=\frac{R}{c}-
\frac{2[t/c+(1-t/c)(b_1/b)]}
{
\ln\!\left[
\frac{R/c^2+(R/c+1)(t/c)-1}
{(R/c)^2-(R/c-1)(t/c)-1}
\right]
+\frac{b_1}{b}\ln\!\left(\frac{R/c-t/c+1}{R/c+t/c-1}\right)
}
$$

$$
k_i=\frac{I_c}{Ac^2(R/c-1)}\frac{1-e/c}{e/c}
$$

where

$$
\frac{I_c}{Ac^2}
=\frac{1}{3}
\frac{1-(1-b_1/b)(1-t/c)^3}
{1-(1-b_1/b)(1-t/c)}-1
$$

$$
k_o=\frac{I_c}{Ac^2(R/c+1)}\frac{1+e/c}{e/c}
$$

*(Note: While $e/c$, $k_i$, and $k_o$ depend upon the width ratio $b_1/b$, they are independent of the width $b$)*

(When $b_1/b=\tfrac{1}{3}$, $t/d=\tfrac{1}{6}$)
