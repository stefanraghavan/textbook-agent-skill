| 2d. Left end fixed, right end fixed

![Figure 8.1-2d: Left end fixed, right end fixed beam with trapezoidal load starting at distance a](figures/fig8_1_2d.png)
<!-- Description: A beam fixed at both ends. A distributed load begins at distance a from the left end and varies linearly from w_a to w_l toward the right end. --> | $$R_A=\frac{w_a}{2l^3}(l-a)^3(l+a)+\frac{w_l-w_a}{20l^3}(l-a)^3(3l+2a)$$

$$M_A=\frac{-w_a}{12l^2}(l-a)^3(l+3a)-\frac{w_l-w_a}{60l^2}(l-a)^3(2l+3a)$$

$$\theta_A=0 \qquad y_A=0$$

$$R_B=\frac{w_a+w_l}{2}(l-a)-R_A$$

$$M_B=R_A l+M_A-\frac{w_a}{2}(l-a)^2-\frac{w_l-w_a}{6l}(l-a)^3$$

$$\theta_B=0 \qquad y_B=0$$ | If $a=0$ and $w_l=w_a$ (uniform load on entire span), then

$$\text{Max }-M=M_A=M_B=\frac{-w_a l^2}{12} \qquad \text{Max }+M=\frac{w_a l^2}{24}\text{ at }x=\frac{l}{2}$$

$$\text{Max }y=\frac{-w_a l^4}{384EI}\text{ at }x=\frac{l}{2}$$

If $a=0$ and $w_a=0$ (uniformly increasing load), then

$$R_A=\frac{3w_l l}{20} \qquad M_A=\frac{-w_l l^2}{30} \qquad R_B=\frac{7w_l l}{20} \qquad \text{Max }-M=M_B=\frac{-w_l l^2}{20}$$

$$\text{Max }+M=0.0215w_l l^2\text{ at }x=0.548l$$

$$\text{Max }y=-0.001309\frac{w_l l^4}{EI}\text{ at }x=0.525l$$ |

| 2e. Left end simply supported, right end simply supported

![Figure 8.1-2e: Left end simply supported, right end simply supported beam with trapezoidal load starting at distance a](figures/fig8_1_2e.png)
<!-- Description: A simply supported beam at both ends. A distributed load begins at distance a from the left end and varies linearly from w_a to w_l toward the right end. --> | $$R_A=\frac{w_a}{2l}(l-a)^2+\frac{w_l-w_a}{6l}(l-a)^2$$

$$M_A=0 \qquad y_A=0$$

$$\theta_A=\frac{-w_a}{24EIl}(l-a)^2(l^2+2al-a^2)$$

$$\qquad -\frac{w_l-w_a}{360EIl}(l-a)^2(7l^2+6al-3a^2)$$

$$R_B=\frac{w_a+w_l}{2}(l-a)-R_A$$

$$\theta_B=\frac{w_a}{24EIl}(l^2-a^2)^2$$

$$\qquad +\frac{w_l-w_a}{360EIl}(l-a)^2(8l^2+9al+3a^2)$$

$$M_B=0 \qquad y_B=0$$ | If $a=0$ and $w_l=w_a$ (uniform load on entire span), then

$$R_A=R_B=\frac{w_a l}{2} \qquad \text{Max }M=\frac{w_a l^2}{8}\text{ at }x=\frac{l}{2}$$

$$\text{Max }\theta=\theta_B=\frac{w_a l^3}{24EI}$$

$$\text{Max }y=\frac{-5w_a l^4}{384EI}\text{ at }x=\frac{l}{2}$$

If $a=0$ and $w_a=0$ (uniformly increasing load), then

$$R_A=\frac{w_l l}{6} \qquad R_B=\frac{w_l l}{3} \qquad \text{Max }M=0.0641w_l l^2\text{ at }x=0.5773l$$

$$\theta_A=\frac{-7w_l l^3}{360EI} \qquad \theta_B=\frac{w_l l^3}{45EI}$$

$$\text{Max }y=-0.00653\frac{w_l l^4}{EI}\text{ at }x=0.5195l$$ |
