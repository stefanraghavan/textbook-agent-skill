# TABLE 8.8 Shear, moment, slope, and deflection formulas for beams under simultaneous axial compression and transverse loading *(Continued)*

## 6c. Left end simply supported, right end fixed

![Figure 6c](figures/fig6c.png)
<!-- Description: Beam with left end simply supported and right end fixed, axial load P, distance a indicated, and temperature labels T1 and T2. -->

$$
M_A = 0 \qquad y_A = 0
$$

$$
R_A = -\frac{\gamma(T_2-T_1)P}{kt}\,\frac{\cos ka-\cos kl}{\sin kl-kl\cos kl}
$$

$$
\theta_A = -\frac{\gamma(T_2-T_1)}{kt}\,\frac{C_3C_{a3}-C_4C_{a2}}{C_2C_3-C_1C_4}
$$

$$
R_B=-R_A \qquad \theta_B=0 \qquad y_B=0 \qquad M_B=R_A l
$$

If $a=0$ (temperature variation over entire span), then

$$
\text{Max }-M=M_B=-\frac{\gamma(T_2-T_1)Pl}{kt}\,\frac{1-\cos kl}{\sin kl-kl\cos kl}
$$

$$
\text{Max }\theta=\theta_A=-\frac{\gamma(T_2-T_1)}{kt}\,\frac{2-2\cos kl-kl\sin kl}{\sin kl-kl\cos kl}
$$

## 6d. Left end fixed, right end fixed

![Figure 6d](figures/fig6d.png)
<!-- Description: Beam fixed at both ends, axial load P, distance a indicated, and temperature labels T1 and T2. -->

$$
\theta_A=0 \qquad y_A=0
$$

$$
R_A=-\frac{\gamma(T_2-T_1)P}{kt}\,\frac{C_3C_{a2}-C_2C_{a3}}{C_3^2-C_2C_4}
$$

$$
M_A=-\frac{\gamma(T_2-T_1)EI}{t}\,\frac{C_3C_{a3}-C_4C_{a2}}{C_3^2-C_2C_4}
$$

$$
R_B=-R_A \qquad \theta_B=0 \qquad y_B=0 \qquad M_B=M_A+R_A l
$$

If $a=0$ (temperature variation over entire span), then

$$
R_A=R_B=0
$$

$$
M=-\frac{\gamma(T_2-T_1)EI}{t}\ \text{everywhere in the span}
$$

$$
\theta=0 \qquad \text{and} \qquad y=0 \ \text{everywhere in the span}
$$

## 6e. Left end simply supported, right end simply supported

![Figure 6e](figures/fig6e.png)
<!-- Description: Beam simply supported at both ends, axial load P, distance a indicated, and temperature labels T1 and T2. -->

$$
R_A=0 \qquad M_A=0 \qquad y_A=0
$$

$$
\theta_A=-\frac{\gamma(T_2-T_1)}{kt}\,\frac{1-\cos k(l-a)}{\sin kl}
$$

$$
R_B=0 \qquad M_B=0 \qquad y_B=0
$$

$$
\theta_B=\frac{\gamma(T_2-T_1)}{kt}\,\frac{\cos ka-\cos kl}{\sin kl}
$$

$$
\text{Max }y \text{ occurs at } x=\frac{1}{k}\tan^{-1}\frac{1-\cos kl\cos ka}{\sin kl};
$$

$$
\text{max possible value}=-\frac{\gamma(T_2-T_1)}{k^2t}\left[\frac{1}{\cos(kl/2)}-1\right] \text{ at } x=\frac{l}{2} \text{ when } a=0
$$

$$
\text{Max }M=P(\text{max }y)
$$

$$
\text{Max }\theta=\theta_B;\ \text{max possible value}=\frac{\gamma(T_2-T_1)}{kt}\tan\frac{kl}{2} \text{ when } a=0
$$

## 6f. Left end guided, right end simply supported

![Figure 6f](figures/fig6f.png)
<!-- Description: Beam with left end guided and right end simply supported, axial load P, distance a indicated, and temperature labels T1 and T2. -->

$$
R_A=0 \qquad \theta_A=0
$$

$$
M_A=\frac{\gamma(T_2-T_1)EI}{t}\,\frac{1-\cos k(l-a)}{\cos kl}
$$

$$
y_A=-\frac{\gamma(T_2-T_1)}{k^2t}\,\frac{1-\cos k(l-a)}{\cos kl}
$$

$$
R_B=0 \qquad M_B=0 \qquad y_B=0
$$

$$
\theta_B=\frac{\gamma(T_2-T_1)}{kt}\,\frac{\sin kl-\sin ka}{\cos kl}
$$

$$
\text{Max }M=M_A;\ \text{max possible value}=\frac{\gamma(T_2-T_1)EI}{t}\left(\frac{1}{\cos kl}-1\right) \text{ when } a=0
$$

$$
\text{Max }y=y_A;\ \text{max possible value}=-\frac{\gamma(T_2-T_1)}{k^2t}\left(\frac{1}{\cos kl}-1\right) \text{ when } a=0
$$

$$
\text{Max }\theta=\theta_B;\ \text{max possible value}=\frac{\gamma(T_2-T_1)}{kt}\tan kl \text{ when } a=0
$$
