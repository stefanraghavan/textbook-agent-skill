| 7. Uniform straight bar under end loads $P$ and end twisting couples $T$; cross section of bar has same $I$ for all central axes; both ends hinged

![Figure 15.1: Uniform straight bar under end loads and end twisting couples](figures/fig15_1.png)
<!-- Description: A vertical straight bar of length $l$ with both ends hinged, axial loads $P$ applied at both ends, and twisting couples $T$ applied at both ends. --> | Critical combination of $P$ and $T$ is given by

$$
\frac{T^2}{4(EI)^2} + \frac{P}{EI} = \frac{\pi^2}{l^2}
$$

(Ref. 1)

If $P = 0$, the formula gives critical twisting moment $T'$ which, acting alone, would cause buckling

If for a given value of $T$ the formula gives a negative value for $P$, $T > T'$ and $P$ represents tensile load required to prevent buckling

For thin circular tube of diameter $D$ and thickness $t$ under torsion only, critical shear stress

$$
\tau = \frac{\pi ED}{l(1-\nu)} \left(1 - \frac{t}{D} + \frac{1}{3}\frac{t^2}{D^2}\right)
$$

for helical buckling only (not for shell-type buckling in the thin wall)

(Ref. 2) |
| 8. Uniform circular ring under uniform radial pressure $p$ lb/in; mean radius of ring $r$

![Figure 15.2: Uniform circular ring under uniform radial pressure](figures/fig15_2.png)
<!-- Description: A circular ring of mean radius $r$ subjected to uniformly distributed radial pressure $p$ acting inward around the circumference. --> | $p' = \dfrac{3EI}{r^3}$

(Ref. 1) |
| 9. Uniform circular arch under uniform radial pressure $p$ lb/in; mean radius $r$; ends hinged

![Figure 15.3: Uniform circular arch under uniform radial pressure, ends hinged](figures/fig15_3.png)
<!-- Description: A circular arch of mean radius $r$ with hinged ends, subtending angle $2\alpha$, subjected to uniform radial pressure $p$. --> | $p' = \dfrac{EI}{r^3}\left(\dfrac{\pi^2}{\alpha^2} - 1\right)$

(Ref. 1)

(For symmetrical arch of any form under central concentrated loading, see Ref. 40; for parabolic and catenary arches, see Ref. 50) |
| 10. Uniform circular arch under uniform radial pressure $p$ lb/in; mean radius $r$; ends fixed

![Figure 15.4: Uniform circular arch under uniform radial pressure, ends fixed](figures/fig15_4.png)
<!-- Description: A circular arch of mean radius $r$ with fixed ends, subtending angle $2\alpha$, subjected to uniform radial pressure $p$. --> | $p' = \dfrac{EI}{r^3}(k^2 - 1)$

(Ref. 1)

where $k$ depends on $\alpha$ and is found by trial from the equation: $k \tan \alpha \cot k\alpha = 1$ or from the following table:

| $\alpha$ | 15° | 30° | 45° | 60° | 75° | 90° | 120° | 180° |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| $k$ | 17.2 | 8.62 | 5.80 | 4.37 | 3.50 | 3.00 | 2.36 | 2.00 |

(For parabolic and catenary arches, see Ref. 50) |
