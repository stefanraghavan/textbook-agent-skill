Ply properties are the same as in the previous example. The constitutive properties are calculated using a standard software package, and are found to be

$$
[A_{11}]_{\text{flange}} = 2.190 \times 10^6 \ \text{lb/in}
$$

$$
[A_{11}]_{\text{web}} = 1.314 \times 10^6 \ \text{lb/in}
$$

The equivalent stiffness is

$$
(EA)^* = \sum bA_{11} = 2[bA_{11}]_{\text{flange}} + [bA_{11}]_{\text{web}} = 2[(1)(2.190 \times 10^6)]
$$

$$
+ [(2)(1.314) \times 10^6)]
$$

$$
= 7.008 \times 10^6 \ \text{lb}
$$

Equation (7.1-3) of Chap. 7 then gives the total beam elongation as

$$
\delta = Pl/(EA)^* = (1000)(20)/(7.008 \times 10^6) = 2.854 \times 10^{-3} \ \text{in}
$$

The transformed stiffness properties, once again from the software package, are $[\bar{Q}_{11}]^0 = 1.974 \times 10^7$ psi and $[\bar{Q}_{11}]^{\pm 30} = 1.203 \times 10^7$ psi. The strain in the beam is simply $\epsilon_x^0 = \delta/l = 1.427 \times 10^{-4}$, giving

$$
[\sigma_x]^0 = 2817 \ \text{psi}
