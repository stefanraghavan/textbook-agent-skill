Calculate the torsional and bending stresses at the wall when a load of 3000 lb is $20^\circ$ out from the wall. Neglect the weight of the beam.

**Solution.** The beam has the following properties: $R = 180\ \text{in};\ \phi = 60^\circ(\pi/3\ \text{rad});\ \theta = 40^\circ;\ \phi - \theta = 20^\circ(\pi/9\ \text{rad});\ I = \frac{1}{12}[4(8^3) - 3.8(7.8^3)] = 20.39\ \text{in}^4;$ $K = 2(0.1^2)(7.9^2)(3.9^2)/[8(0.1) + 4(0.1) - 2(0.1^2)] = 16.09\ \text{in}^4$ (see Table 10.1, case 16); $E = 30(10^6);\ G = 12(10^6);\text{ and }\beta = 30(10^6)(20.39)/12(10^6)(16.09) = 3.168.$ Equations for a curved beam that is fixed at one end and simply supported at the other with a concentrated load are found in Table 9.4, case 1b. To obtain the bending and twisting moments at the wall requires first the evaluation of the end reaction $V_A$, which, in turn, requires the following constants:

$$
C_3 = -3.168\left(\frac{\pi}{3} - \sin 60^\circ\right) - \frac{1 + 3.168}{2}\left(\frac{\pi}{3}\cos 60^\circ - \sin 60^\circ\right) = 0.1397
$$

$$
C_{a3} = -3.168\left(\frac{\pi}{9} - \sin 20^\circ\right) - C_{a2} = 0.006867
$$

Similarly,

$$
C_6 = C_1 = 0.3060,\qquad C_{a6} = C_{a1} = 0.05775
$$

$$
C_9 = C_2 = -0.7136,\qquad C_{a9} = C_{a2} = -0.02919
$$

Therefore,

$$
V_A = 3000\frac{-0.02919(1 - \cos 60^\circ) - 0.05775\sin 60^\circ + 0.006867}{-0.7136(1 - \cos 60^\circ) - 0.3060\sin 60^\circ + 0.1397} = 359.3\ \text{lb}
$$

$$
M_B = 359.3(180)(\sin 60^\circ) - 3000(180)(\sin 20^\circ) = -128{,}700\ \text{lb-in}
$$

$$
T_B = 359.3(180)(1 - \cos 60^\circ) - 3000(180)(1 - \cos 20^\circ) = -230\ \text{lb-in}
$$

At the wall,

$$
\sigma = \frac{Mc}{I} = \frac{128{,}700(4)}{20.39} = 25{,}240\ \text{lb/in}^2
$$

$$
\tau =
\begin{cases}
\frac{V_A'\bar{y}}{Ib} = \frac{(3000 - 359.3)[4(4)(2) - 3.9(3.8)(1.95)]}{20.39(0.2)} = 2008\ \text{lb/in}^2 & \text{(due to transverse shear)} \\
\\
\frac{T}{2t(a - t)(b - t)} = \frac{230}{2(0.1)(7.9)(3.9)} = 37.3\ \text{lb/in}^2 & \text{(due to torsion)}
\end{cases}
$$
