# TABLE 9.3 Reaction and deformation formulas for circular arches *(Continued)*

**9. Left end roller supported in vertical direction only, right end fixed**

![Figure 9.3-9: Left end roller supported in vertical direction only, right end fixed](figures/fig9_3_9.png)
<!-- Description: Circular arch with a roller support at the left end providing vertical reaction $V_A$, and the right end fixed. -->

Since $\delta_{VA}=0$, $H_A=0$, and $M_A=0$,

$$
V_A=\frac{LF_V}{B_{VV}}, \qquad \delta_{HA}=\frac{R^3}{EI}(B_{HV}V_A-LF_H)
$$

$$
\psi_A=\frac{R^2}{EI}(B_{MV}V_A-LF_M)
$$

Use load terms given above for cases 5a–5s

**10. Left end roller supported in horizontal direction only, right end fixed**

![Figure 9.3-10: Left end roller supported in horizontal direction only, right end fixed](figures/fig9_3_10.png)
<!-- Description: Circular arch with a roller support at the left end providing horizontal reaction $H_A$, and the right end fixed. -->

Since $\delta_{HA}=0$, $V_A=0$, and $M_A=0$,

$$
H_A=\frac{LF_H}{B_{HH}}, \qquad \delta_{VA}=\frac{R^3}{EI}(B_{VH}H_A-LF_V)
$$

$$
\psi_A=\frac{R^2}{EI}(B_{MH}H_A-LF_M)
$$

Use load terms given above for cases 5a–5s

---

**11. Left end restrained against rotation only, right end fixed**

![Figure 9.3-11: Left end restrained against rotation only, right end fixed](figures/fig9_3_11.png)
<!-- Description: Circular arch with the left end restrained against rotation only, producing moment reaction $M_A$, and the right end fixed. -->

Since $\psi_A=0$, $H_A=0$, and $V_A=0$,

$$
\frac{M_A}{R}=\frac{LF_M}{B_{MM}}, \qquad \delta_{HA}=\frac{R^3}{EI}\left(B_{HM}\frac{M_A}{R}-LF_H\right)
$$

$$
\delta_{VA}=\frac{R^3}{EI}\left(B_{VM}\frac{M_A}{R}-LF_V\right)
$$

Use load terms given above for cases 5a–5s

---

**12. Left end free, right end fixed**

![Figure 9.3-12: Left end free, right end fixed](figures/fig9_3_12.png)
<!-- Description: Circular arch with the left end free and the right end fixed. -->

Since $H_A=0$, $V_A=0$, and $M_A=0$, this is a statically determinate problem. The deflections at the free end are given by

$$
\delta_{HA}=-\frac{R^3}{EI}LF_H, \qquad \delta_{VA}=-\frac{R^3}{EI}LF_V
$$

$$
\psi_A=-\frac{R^2}{EI}LF_M
$$

Use load terms given above for cases 5a–5s
