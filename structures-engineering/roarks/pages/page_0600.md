| **2b.** Filled to depth $d$ with liquid of density $\delta$ force/unit volume; tangential edge support | At any level $y$ below the liquid surface $y \le d$ |

![Figure 13.1: Conical vessel filled to depth d with dimension y and tangential edge support](figures/fig13_1.png)

$$
\sigma_1=\frac{\delta y\tan\alpha}{2t\cos\alpha}\left(d-\frac{2y}{3}\right), \qquad
(\sigma_1)_{\max}=\frac{3\delta d^2\tan\alpha}{16t\cos\alpha}
\quad \text{when } y=\frac{3d}{4}
$$

$$
\sigma_2=\frac{y(d-y)\delta\tan\alpha}{t\cos\alpha}, \qquad
(\sigma_2)_{\max}=\frac{\delta d^2\tan\alpha}{4t\cos\alpha}
\quad \text{when } y=\frac{d}{2}
$$

$$
\Delta R=\frac{\delta y^2\tan^2\alpha}{Et\cos\alpha}\left[d\left(1-\frac{\nu}{2}\right)-y\left(1-\frac{\nu}{3}\right)\right]
$$

$$
\Delta y=\frac{\delta y^2\sin\alpha}{Et\cos^4\alpha}
\left\{
\frac{d}{4}(1-2\nu)-\frac{y}{9}(1-3\nu)
-\sin^2\alpha\left[\frac{d}{2}(2-\nu)-\frac{y}{3}(3-\nu)\right]
\right\}
$$

$$
\psi=\frac{\delta y\sin^2\alpha}{6Et\cos^3\alpha}(9d-16y)
$$

At any level $y$ above the liquid level

$$
\sigma_1=\frac{\delta d^3\tan\alpha}{6ty\cos\alpha}, \qquad
\sigma_2=0, \qquad
\Delta R=\frac{-\nu\delta d^3\tan^2\alpha}{6Et\cos\alpha}
$$

$$
\Delta y=\frac{\delta d^3\sin\alpha}{6Et\cos^4\alpha}
\left[
\frac{5}{6}-\nu(1-\sin^2\alpha)+\ln\frac{y}{d}
\right]
$$

$$
\psi=\frac{-\delta d^3\sin^2\alpha}{6Et\cos^3\alpha}\frac{1}{y}
$$

(Note: There is a discontinuity in the rate of increase in fluid pressure at the top of the liquid. This leads to some bending in this region and is indicated by a discrepancy in the two expressions for the meridional slope at $y=d$.)

$$
\sigma_2=\delta R\tan\alpha

$$

$$
\Delta y=\frac{\delta R^2}{E\cos^2\alpha}\left(\frac{1}{4\sin^2\alpha}-\sin^2\alpha\right)
$$

$$
\psi=\frac{2\delta R}{E\cos^2\alpha}\left[\sin^2\alpha\left(1+\frac{\nu}{2}\right)-\frac{1}{4}(1+2\nu)\right]
$$
