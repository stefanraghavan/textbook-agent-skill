# TABLE 12.1 Formulas for short prisms loaded eccentrically; stress reversal impossible *(Continued)*

4. Solid circular section

![Figure 12.1: Solid circular section](figures/fig12_1.png)
<!-- Description: Circular section diagram showing radius r, eccentricity e, neutral axis N-N, angle ϕ, and distance x. -->

$$
m=\frac{r}{4}
$$

$$
x=r(1-\sin \phi)\qquad \text{where } \phi \text{ satisfies}\qquad \frac{e}{r}=\frac{\frac{1}{8}\pi-\frac{1}{4}\phi-\frac{5}{8}\sin\phi\cos\phi+\frac{1}{2}\sin^3\phi\cos\phi}{\cos\phi-\frac{1}{3}\cos^3\phi-\frac{1}{2}\pi\sin\phi+\phi\sin\phi}
$$

$$
\sigma_{\max}=\frac{P}{A}\,\frac{\pi(1-\sin\phi)}{\cos\phi-\frac{1}{3}\cos^3\phi-\frac{1}{2}\pi\sin\phi+\phi\sin\phi}
\qquad \text{or} \qquad
\sigma_{\max}=\frac{P}{A}K
$$

where $K$ is given by following table:

m=\frac{\frac{1}{4}\left(r^2+r_1^2\right)}{r}
$$

$$
\sigma_{\max}=\frac{P}{A}K
$$
