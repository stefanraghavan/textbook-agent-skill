Using the equations given in Table 6.1 at the end of the chapter,

$$
\frac{\varepsilon_A + \varepsilon_C}{1 - \nu} = \frac{200 + 1000}{1 - 0.285} = 1678.3\,\mu
$$

$$
\frac{1}{1 + \nu}\sqrt{(\varepsilon_A - \varepsilon_C)^2 + (2\varepsilon_B - \varepsilon_A - \varepsilon_C)^2}
$$

$$
= \frac{1}{1 + 0.285}\sqrt{(200 - 1000)^2 + [2(900) - 200 - 1000]^2} = 778.2\,\mu
$$

Thus,

$$
\sigma_{p1} = \frac{200(10)^9}{2}(1678.3 + 778.2) = 245.65\ \text{MPa}
$$

$$
\sigma_{p2} = \frac{200(10)^9}{2}(1678.3 - 778.2) = 90.01\ \text{MPa}
$$

The principal angle is

$$
\theta_p = \frac{1}{2}\tan^{-1}\left(\frac{2(900) - 200 - 1000}{200 - 1000}\right) = \frac{1}{2}\tan^{-1}\left(\frac{+600}{-800}\right) = \frac{1}{2}(143.13^\circ) = 71.6^\circ
$$

counterclockwise from the $x$ axis ($A$ gage) to $\sigma_{p1} = 245.65$ MPa.$\dagger$ Note that this agrees with Fig. 6.4(b).

---

$\dagger$ When calculating $\theta_p$, do not change the signs of the numerator and denominator in the equation. The $\tan^{-1}$ is defined from $0^\circ$ to $360^\circ$. For example, $\tan^{-1}(+/+)$ is in the range $0^\circ$-$90^\circ$, $\tan^{-1}(+/-)$ is in the range $90^\circ$-$180^\circ$, $\tan^{-1}(-/-)$ is in the range $180^\circ$-$270^\circ$, and $\tan^{-1}(-/+)$ is in the range $270^\circ$-$360^\circ$. Using this definition for $\theta_p$, the calculation will yield the counterclockwise angle from the $x$ axis to $\sigma_{p1}$, the greater of the two principal stresses in the plane of the gages.
