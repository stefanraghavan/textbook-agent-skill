# TABLE 13.4 Formulas for discontinuity stresses and deformations at the junctions of shells and plates *(Continued)*

**4b. Axial load $P$**

![Figure 13.4: Axial load P at junction of shell and plate](figures/fig13_4.png)
<!-- Description: Sketch of a cylindrical shell connected to a plate at the right end, showing axial load P, radius R2, and displacement w2. -->

$$
w_2=\frac{P}{2\pi R_2}
$$

$$
LT_{A1}=-\frac{\nu_1 R_1^2}{2t_1^2}, \qquad LT_{AC}=0
$$

$$
LT_{A2}=\frac{E_1 t_2 R_1^3}{16D_2 t_1}K_{P2}
$$

where

$$
K_{P2}=
\begin{cases}
\left(1-\dfrac{R_2^2}{R_1^2}\right)K_{P1} & \text{for } R_2 \leq R_1 \\\\
-\dfrac{1-\nu_2}{1+\nu_2}\dfrac{R_2^2-R_1^2}{a_2^2}-2\ln\dfrac{R_2}{R_1} & \text{for } R_2 \geq R_1
\end{cases}
$$

$$
LT_{B1}=0, \qquad LT_{BC}=0
$$

$$
LT_{B2}=\frac{E_1 R_1^3}{8D_2}K_{P2}
$$

At the junction of the cylinder and plate,

$$
V_1=\frac{Pt_1K_{V1}}{\pi R_1^2}, \qquad
M_1=\frac{Pt_1^2K_{M1}}{\pi R_1^2}, \qquad
N_1=\frac{P}{2\pi R_1}
$$

$$
\Delta R_A=\frac{Pt_1}{E_1\pi R_1^2}\left(LT_{A1}-K_{V1}C_{AA1}-K_{M1}C_{AB1}\right)
$$

$$
\psi_A=\frac{P}{E_1\pi R_1^2}\left(K_{V1}C_{AB1}+K_{M1}C_{BB1}\right)
$$

For axial tension, $E_1=E_2$, $\nu_1=\nu_2=0.3$, $a_2=a_1$, and for $R_1/t_1>5$ and $R_1/t_2>4$.

$$
\Delta R_A=\frac{P\nu_1}{2\pi E_1 t_1}K_{\Delta RA}, \qquad
\psi_A=\frac{P\nu_1}{2\pi E_1 t_1^2}K_{\psi A}, \qquad
\sigma_2=\frac{P}{2\pi R_1 t_1}K_{\sigma 2}
$$
