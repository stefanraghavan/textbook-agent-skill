# TABLE 13.4 Formulas for discontinuity stresses and deformations at the junctions of shells and plates *(Continued)*

**1c. Hydrostatic internal\* pressure**  
$q_1$ at the junction for $x_1 > 3/\lambda_1$†

![Figure 13.4: hydrostatic internal pressure at the junction](figures/fig13_4.png)
<!-- Description: Sketch of two joined cylindrical shells with internal pressure q1 acting near the junction, showing coordinate x1 and tapered pressure distribution arrows. -->

*Note:* There is no axial load on the left cylinder. A small axial load on the right cylinder balances any axial pressure on the joint.

$$
LT_{A1}=\frac{b_1R_1}{t_1^2}
$$

$$
LT_{A2}=\frac{-b_2R_2E_1}{E_2t_1t_2}
$$

For $LT_{AC}$ use the expression from case 1a

$$
LT_{B1}=\frac{-b_1R_1}{x_1t_1}, \qquad LT_{B2}=\frac{b_2R_2E_1}{x_1E_2t_2}
$$

For $LT_{BC}$ use the expression from case 1a

At the junction of the two cylinders,

$$
V_1=q_1t_1K_{V1}, \qquad M_1=q_1t_1^2K_{M1}, \qquad N_1=0
$$

$$
\Delta R_A=\frac{q_1t_1}{E_1}(LT_{A1}-K_{V1}C_{AA1}-K_{M1}C_{AB1})
$$

$$
\psi_A=\frac{q_1}{E_1}(-LT_{B1}+K_{V1}C_{AB1}+K_{M1}C_{BB1})
$$

For internal pressure, $b_1=b_2$ (smooth internal wall), $E_1=E_2$, $\nu_1=\nu_2=0.3$, and for $R/t > 5$.

$$
\Delta R_A=\frac{q_1R_1^2}{E_1t_1^1}K_{\Delta RA}, \qquad
\psi_A=\frac{q_1R_1}{E_1t_1^1}K_{\psi A}, \qquad
\sigma_2=\frac{q_1R_1}{t_1}K_{\sigma 2}
$$
