# TABLE 8.8 Shear, moment, slope, and deflection formulas for beams under simultaneous axial compression and transverse loading *(Continued)*

**2d. Left end fixed, right end fixed**

![Figure 2d](figures/fig2d.png)
<!-- Description: Beam with left end fixed and right end fixed, axial compressive load P at the left, distance a to start of distributed load varying from w_a to w_l near the right end. -->

$\theta_A = 0 \qquad y_A = 0$

$$
R_A=\frac{w_a}{k}\frac{C_3C_{a4}-C_2C_{a5}}{C_3^2-C_2C_4}
+\frac{w_l-w_a}{k^2(l-a)}\frac{C_3C_{a5}-C_2C_{a6}}{C_3^2-C_2C_4}
$$

$$
M_A=\frac{w_a}{k^2}\frac{C_4C_{a4}-C_3C_{a5}}{C_3^2-C_2C_4}
-\frac{w_l-w_a}{k^3(l-a)}\frac{C_4C_{a5}-C_3C_{a6}}{C_3^2-C_2C_4}
$$

$$
R_B=\frac{w_a+w_l}{2}(l-a)-R_A
$$

$$
M_B=M_A+R_A l-\frac{w_a}{2}(l-a)^2-\frac{w_l-w_a}{6}(l-a)^2
$$

$$
\theta_B=0 \qquad y_B=0
$$

If $a=0$ and $w_a=w_l$ (uniform load on entire span), then

$$
\text{Max }-M=M_A=M_B=-\frac{w_a}{k^2}\left[1-\frac{kl/2}{\tan(kl/2)}\right]
$$

$$
\text{Max }+M=\frac{w_a}{k^2}\left[\frac{kl/2}{\sin(kl/2)}-1\right] \qquad \text{at } x=\frac{l}{2}
$$

$$
\text{Max }y=-\frac{w_a l}{2kP}\left(\tan\frac{kl}{4}-\frac{kl}{4}\right) \qquad \text{at } x=\frac{l}{2}
$$

$$
R_A=R_B=\frac{w_a l}{2}
$$

If $a=0$ and $w_a=0$ (uniformly increasing load), then

$$
\text{Max }-M=M_B=\frac{w_l}{k^2}\left[1-\frac{(kl/2)\sin kl-k^2l^2/6-(k^2l^2/3)\cos kl}{2-2\cos kl-kl\sin kl}\right]
$$

$$
M_A=\frac{w_l l}{6k}\frac{3\sin kl-kl(2+\cos kl)}{2-2\cos kl-kl\sin kl}
$$

$$
R_A=\frac{w_l}{k^2 l}\left(\frac{k^2l^2/3-3\cos kl-kl\sin kl}{6}\frac{}{\,2-2\cos kl-kl\sin kl}-1\right)
$$

**2e. Left end simply supported, right end simply supported**

![Figure 2e](figures/fig2e.png)
<!-- Description: Beam simply supported at both ends, axial compressive load P at the left, distance a to start of distributed load varying from w_a to w_l toward the right. -->

$$
M_A=0 \qquad y_A=0
$$

$$
R_A=\frac{w_a}{2l}(l-a)^2+\frac{w_l-w_a}{6l}(l-a)^2
$$

$$
\theta_A=-\frac{w_a}{kP}\left[\frac{1-\cos k(l-a)}{\sin kl}-\frac{k}{2l}(l-a)^2\right]
$$

$$
-\frac{w_l-w_a}{kP}\left[\frac{k(l-a)-\sin k(l-a)}{k(l-a)\sin kl}-\frac{k}{6l}(l-a)^2\right]
$$

$$
R_B=\frac{w_a+w_l}{2}(l-a)-R_A
$$

$$
M_B=0 \qquad y_B=0
$$

$$
\theta_B=\frac{w_a}{kP}\left[\frac{\cos ka-\cos kl}{\sin kl}-\frac{k(l^2-a^2)}{2l}\right]
$$

$$
+\frac{w_l-w_a}{k^2P(l-a)}\left[\frac{k^2}{6l}(3al^2-2l^3-a^3)+1-\frac{\sin ka+k(l-a)\cos kl}{\sin kl}\right]
$$

If $a=0$ and $w_a=w_l$ (uniform load on entire span), then

$$
\text{Max }+M=\frac{w_a}{k^2}\left[\frac{1}{\cos(kl/2)}-1\right] \qquad \text{at } x=\frac{l}{2}
$$

$$
\text{Max }\theta=\theta_B=-\theta_A=\frac{w_a}{kP}\left(\tan\frac{kl}{2}-\frac{kl}{2}\right)
$$

$$
\text{Max }y=-\frac{w_a}{k^2P}\left[\frac{1}{\cos(kl/2)}-\frac{k^2l^2}{8}-1\right] \qquad \text{at } x=\frac{l}{2}
$$

If $a=0$ and $w_a=0$ (uniformly increasing load), then

$$
M=\frac{w_l}{k^2}\left(\frac{\sin kx}{\sin kl}-\frac{x}{l}\right); \quad \max M \text{ occurs at } x=\frac{1}{k}\cos^{-1}\frac{\sin kl}{kl}
$$

$$
\theta_A=-\frac{w_l}{kP}\left(\frac{1}{\sin kl}-\frac{1}{kl}-\frac{kl}{6}\right)
$$

$$
\text{Max }\theta=\theta_B=\frac{w_l}{kP}\left(\frac{1}{kl}-\frac{kl}{3}-\frac{1}{\tan kl}\right)
$$

$$
y=-\frac{w_l}{k^2P}\left[\frac{\sin kx}{\sin kl}-\frac{x}{l}-\frac{k^2x}{6l}(l^2-x^2)\right]
$$
