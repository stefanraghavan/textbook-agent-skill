# TABLE 13.4  Formulas for discontinuity stresses and deformations at the junctions of shells and plates *(Continued)*

**8b. Axial load $P$**

![Figure 13.4: Axial load P at the junction of two spheres](figures/fig13_4.png)
<!-- Description: Two joined spherical shells with axial load P applied outward along the horizontal axis on both sides. -->

$$
LT_{A1}=\frac{-R_1^2(1+\nu_1)}{2t_1^2\sin\phi_1}
$$

$$
LT_{A2}=\frac{R_1^2E_1(1+\nu_2)}{2E_2t_1t_2\sin\phi_2}
+\frac{R_1C_{AA2}}{2t_1\sin\phi_1}\left(\frac{1}{\tan\phi_1}+\frac{1}{\tan\phi_2}\right)
$$

$$
LT_{AC}=0*
$$

$$
LT_{B1}=0
$$

$$
LT_{B2}=\frac{R_1C_{AB2}}{2t_1\sin\phi_1}\left(\frac{1}{\tan\phi_1}+\frac{1}{\tan\phi_2}\right)
$$

$$
LT_{BC}=0*
$$

At the junction of the two spheres,

$$
V_1=\frac{Pt_1K_{V1}}{\pi R_1^2}
$$

$$
M_1=\frac{Pt_1^2K_{M1}}{\pi R_1^2}
$$

$$
N_1=\frac{P}{2\pi R_1\sin^2\phi_1}-V_1\cos\phi_1
$$

$$
\Delta R_A=\frac{Pt_1}{E_1\pi R_1^2}\left(LT_{A1}-K_{V1}C_{AA1}-K_{M1}C_{AB1}\right)
$$

$$
\psi_A=\frac{P}{E_1\pi R_1^2}\left(-LT_{B1}+K_{V1}C_{AB1}+K_{M1}C_{BB1}\right)
$$

For axial tension, $E_1=E_2$, $\nu_1=\nu_2=0.3$, $t_1=t_2$, $R_1\sin\phi_1=R_2\sin\phi_2$, and for $R/t>5$.

$$
\Delta R_A=\frac{P\nu_1}{2\pi E_1t_1}K_{\Delta RA},
\qquad
\psi_A=\frac{P\nu_1}{2\pi E_1t_1^2}K_{\psi A},
\qquad
\sigma_2=\frac{P}{2\pi R_1t_1}K_{\sigma 2}
$$
