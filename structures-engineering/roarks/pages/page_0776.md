## SEC. 17.1] Stress Concentration 773

The *static stress concentration factor* in the elastic range, $K_t$, is defined as the ratio of the maximum stress, $\sigma_{\max}$, to the nominal stress, $\sigma_{\mathrm{nom}}$. That is,

$$
K_t = \frac{\sigma_{\max}}{\sigma_{\mathrm{nom}}}
\qquad (17.1\text{-}2)
$$

For the infinite plate containing a hole and loaded in tension, $\sigma_{\mathrm{nom}} = \sigma$, $\sigma_{\max} = 3\sigma$, and thus $K_t = 3.*$

The analysis of the plate in tension with a hole just given is for a very wide plate (infinite in the limit). As the width of the plate decreases, the maximum stress becomes less than three times the nominal stress at the zone containing the hole. Figure 17.3(a) shows a plate of thickness $t = 0.125$ in, width $D = 1.50$ in, with a hole of diameter $2r = 0.50$ in, and an applied uniform stress of $\sigma_0 = 320$ psi.
