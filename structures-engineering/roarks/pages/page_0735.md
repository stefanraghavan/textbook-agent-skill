| **2. Rectangular plate under uniform compression (or tension) $\sigma_x$ on edges $b$ and uniform compression (or tension) $\sigma_y$ on edges $a$** | **2a. All edges simply supported** | $\sigma_x' \dfrac{m^2}{a^2} + \sigma_y' \dfrac{n^2}{b^2} = 0.823 \dfrac{E}{1-\nu^2} t^2 \left(\dfrac{m^2}{a^2} + \dfrac{n^2}{b^2}\right)^2$ |

![Figure 2: Rectangular plate under uniform compression (or tension)](figures/fig2.png)
<!-- Description: A rectangular plate with inward horizontal stresses $\sigma_x$ on the two vertical sides and vertical stresses $\sigma_y$ on the top and bottom sides. -->

Here $m$ and $n$ signify the number of half-waves in the buckled plate in the $x$ and $y$ directions, respectively. To find $\sigma_y'$ for a given $\sigma_x$, take $m = 1$, $n = 1$ if

$$
C\left(1 - 4\dfrac{a^4}{b^4}\right) < \sigma_x < C\left(5 + 2\dfrac{a^2}{b^2}\right),
$$

where

$$
C = \dfrac{0.823Et^2}{(1-\nu^2)a^2}.
$$

If $\sigma_x$ is too large to satisfy this inequality, take $n = 1$ and $m$ to satisfy:

$$
C\left(2m^2 - 2m + 1 + 2\dfrac{a^2}{b^2}\right) < \sigma_x < C\left(2m^2 + 2m + 1 + 2\dfrac{a^2}{b^2}\right).
$$

If $\sigma_x$ is too small to satisfy the first inequality, take $m = 1$ and $n$ to satisfy:

$$
C\left[1 - n^2(n-1)^2\dfrac{a^4}{b^4}\right] > \sigma_x > C\left[1 - n^2(n+1)^2\dfrac{a^4}{b^4}\right]
$$

(Refs. 1, 6)
