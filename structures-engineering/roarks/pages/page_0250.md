# TABLE 8.10 Beams restrained against horizontal displacement at the ends

| Case no., manner of loading and support | Formulas to solve for $y_{\max}$ and $P$ |
|---|---|
| 1. Ends pinned to rigid supports, concentrated center load $W$ | $y_{\max} + \dfrac{A}{4I} y_{\max}^3 = \dfrac{2Wl^3}{\pi^4 EI}$  (Solve for $y_{\max}$)  <br><br> $P = \dfrac{\pi^2 EA}{4l^2} y_{\max}^2$  <br><br> Use case 1e from Table 8.7(b) or Table 8.9 to determine maximum slopes and moments after solving for $P$ |
| 2. Ends fixed to rigid supports, concentrated center load $W$ | $y_{\max} + \dfrac{A}{16I} y_{\max}^3 = \dfrac{Wl^3}{2\pi^4 EI}$  (Solve for $y_{\max}$)  <br><br> $P = \dfrac{\pi^2 EA}{4l^2} y_{\max}^2$  <br><br> Use case 1d from Table 8.7(d) or Table 8.9 to determine maximum slopes and moments after solving for $P$ |
| 3. Ends pinned to rigid supports, uniformly distributed transverse load $w$ on entire span | $y_{\max} + \dfrac{A}{4I} y_{\max}^3 = \dfrac{5wl^4}{4\pi^4 EI}$  (Solve for $y_{\max}$)  <br><br> $P = \dfrac{\pi^2 EA}{4l^2} y_{\max}^2$  <br><br> Use case 2e from Table 8.7(b) or Table 8.9 to determine maximum slopes and moments after solving for $P$ |
| 4. Ends fixed to rigid supports, uniformly distributed transverse load $w$ on entire span | $y_{\max} + \dfrac{A}{16I} y_{\max}^3 = \dfrac{wl^4}{4\pi^4 EI}$  (Solve for $y_{\max}$)  <br><br> $P = \dfrac{\pi^2 EA}{4l^2} y_{\max}^2$  <br><br> Use case 2d from Table 8.7(d) or Table 8.9 to determine maximum slopes and moments after solving for $P$ |
| 5. Same as case 1, except beam is perfectly flexible like a cable or chain and has an unstretched length $l$ | $\tan \theta - \sin \theta = \dfrac{W}{2EA}$ or if $\theta < 12^\circ$, $\theta = \left(\dfrac{W}{EA}\right)^{1/3}$  <br><br> $P = \dfrac{W}{2\tan\theta}$  <br><br> ![Figure 8.10: cable with concentrated center load](figures/fig8_10.png)  <!-- Description: A flexible cable or chain of span l between two supports, with horizontal end forces P, a concentrated center load W, and end angle theta shown on both sides. --> |
| 6. Same as case 3, except beam is perfectly flexible like a cable or chain and has an unstretched length $l$ | $y_{\max} = l \left(\dfrac{3wl}{64EA}\right)^{1/3}$  <br><br> $P = \dfrac{wl^2}{8y_{\max}}$  <br><br> ![Figure 8.10: cable with uniformly distributed load](figures/fig8_10.png)  <!-- Description: A flexible cable or chain of span l between two supports under uniformly distributed load w lb/in, with horizontal end forces P and a sagging curve. --> |
