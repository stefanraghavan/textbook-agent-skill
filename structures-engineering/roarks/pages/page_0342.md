# TABLE 9.3 Reaction and deformation formulas for circular arches *(Continued)*

## 1k. Partial uniformly increasing distributed radial loading

![Figure 1k: Partial uniformly increasing distributed radial loading](figures/fig1k.png)
<!-- Description: A circular arch segment with a partially loaded top region, radial arrows indicating increasing distributed load $w$, and angle $\phi$ marked from the vertical centerline. -->

$$
LP_H=\frac{wR}{\theta+\phi}\left\{\theta c(\theta+\phi-cn-sm)+\frac{k_1}{2}\left[n(sc-\theta-\phi)+s^2m+2c-2m\right]\right.
$$

$$
\left.+\,k_2\left[scn+s^2m-\frac{c}{2}(\theta+\phi)^2+c-m\right]\right\}
$$

$$
LP_M=\frac{wR}{\theta+\phi}\left\{\frac{\theta}{2}(cn+sm-\theta-\phi)+\frac{k_1}{4s^2}\left[(\theta+\phi)(\theta+sc)+\phi sm-\theta cn\right.\right.
$$

$$
\left.\left.-\,2s^2-2sn\right]+\frac{k_2}{4}\left[(\theta+\phi)^2+2cm-2sn-2\right]\right\}
$$

If $\phi=\theta$ (the full span is loaded)

$$
LP_H=\frac{wRc}{\theta}\left[\theta(\theta-cs)-\frac{k_1s}{2c}(\theta-sc)-k_2(\theta^2-s^2)\right]
$$

$$
LP_M=\frac{wR}{2\theta}\left[\theta sc-\theta^2+\frac{k_1}{2s^2}(\theta^2+\theta sc-2s^2)+k_2(\theta^2-s^2)\right]
$$

For $\alpha=\beta=0$

| $\theta$ | 30° | 30° | 60° | 60° | 90° | 90° |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| $\phi$ | 0° | 15° | 0° | 30° | 0° | 45° |
| $\dfrac{LP_H}{wR}$ | -0.0018 | -0.0035 | -0.0518 | -0.0915 | -0.3183 | -0.5036 |
| $\dfrac{LP_M}{wR}$ | -0.0072 | -0.0142 | -0.0516 | -0.0968 | -0.1366 | -0.2335 |

## 1l. Partial second-order increase in distributed radial loading

![Figure 1l: Partial second-order increase in distributed radial loading](figures/fig1l.png)
<!-- Description: A circular arch segment with a partially loaded top region, radial arrows indicating second-order increasing distributed load $w$, and angle $\phi$ marked from the vertical centerline. -->

$$
LP_H=\frac{wRc}{(\theta+\phi)^2}\left\{\theta(\theta+\phi)^2-2\theta(1-cm+sn)+\frac{k_1}{c}\left[(\theta+\phi)(2c+m)\right.\right.
$$

$$
\left.\left.-\,c(sm+cn)-2n-2s\right]+\frac{k_2}{3}\left[6(\theta+\phi-sm-cn)-(\theta+\phi)^3\right]\right\}
$$

$$
LP_M=\frac{wR}{(\theta+\phi)^2}\left\{\phi(1-cm+sn)-\frac{\theta}{2}(\theta+\phi)^2\right.
$$

$$
+\frac{k_1}{4s^2}\left[(\theta+\phi)^2(\theta+sc)+2s(\phi n+3m-3c)-4s^2(\theta+\phi)-2\theta(1-cm)\right]
$$

$$
\left.+\,k_2\left[sm+cn-\theta-\phi+\frac{(\theta+\phi)^3}{6}\right]\right\}
$$

If $\phi=\theta$ (the full span is loaded)

$$
LP_H=\frac{wRc}{6\theta^2}\left[6\theta^3c-6\theta s^2c+3k_1(3\theta c-3s+s^3)+2k_2(3\theta c-2\theta^3c-3sc^2)\right]
$$

$$
LP_M=\frac{wR}{2\theta^2}\left[\theta s^2-\theta^3+k_1\frac{\theta}{2s^2}(\theta^2+\theta sc-2s^2)+k_2\left(sc-\theta+\frac{2\theta^3}{3}\right)\right]
$$

For $\alpha=\beta=0$

| $\theta$ | 30° | 30° | 60° | 60° | 90° | 90° |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| $\phi$ | 0° | 15° | 0° | 30° | 0° | 45° |
| $\dfrac{LP_H}{wR}$ | -0.0010 | -0.0019 | -0.0276 | -0.0532 | -0.1736 | -0.3149 |
| $\dfrac{LP_M}{wR}$ | -0.0037 | -0.0077 | -0.0269 | -0.0542 | -0.0726 | -0.1388 |
