## 2. Cylinder of length $L$ large as compared with $D$; $p =$ load per unit length $= P/L$

![Figure 2: Cylinder of length L large as compared with D](figures/fig2.png)
<!-- Description: A cylinder of diameter D₂ pressed vertically by load P against a flat surface, with contact width b indicated at the base. -->

$$
b = 1.60\sqrt{pK_DC_E}
$$

$$
(\sigma_c)_{\max} = 0.798\sqrt{\frac{p}{K_DC_E}}
$$

If $E_1 = E_2 = E$ and $v_1 = v_2 = 0.3$, then

$$
b = 2.15\sqrt{\frac{pK_D}{E}}
$$

$$
(\sigma_c)_{\max} = 0.591\sqrt{\frac{pE}{K_D}}
$$

For a cylinder between two flat plates

$$
\Delta D_2 = \frac{4p(1-v^2)}{\pi E}\left(\frac{1}{3} + \ln \frac{2D}{b}\right)
$$

Refs. 5 and 44

For a cylinder on a cylinder the distance between centers is reduced by

$$
\frac{2p(1-v^2)}{\pi E}\left(\frac{2}{3} + \ln \frac{2D_1}{b} + \ln \frac{2D_2}{b}\right)
$$

Ref. 31

For graphs of subsurface stress variations see Refs. 6 and 56

### 2a. Cylinder on a flat plate

![Figure 2a: Cylinder on a flat plate](figures/fig2_1.png)
<!-- Description: A cylinder pressed onto a flat plate by a vertical load, showing the contact with the plane. -->

$$
K_D = D_2
$$

$$
\tau_{\max} \approx \frac{1}{3}(\sigma_c)_{\max}
$$

at a depth of $0.4b$ below the surface of the plane

### 2b. Cylinder on a cylinder

![Figure 2b: Cylinder on a cylinder](figures/fig2_2.png)
<!-- Description: Two cylinders in vertical contact with diameters D₂ and D₁ indicated. -->

$$
K_D = \frac{D_1D_2}{D_1 + D_2}
$$

### 2c. Cylinder in a cylindrical socket

![Figure 2c: Cylinder in a cylindrical socket](figures/fig2_3.png)
<!-- Description: A cylinder of diameter D₂ seated inside a concave cylindrical socket of diameter D₁. -->

$$
K_D = \frac{D_1D_2}{D_1 - D_2}
$$

## 3. Cylinder on a cylinder; axes at right angles

![Figure 3: Cylinder on a cylinder, axes at right angles](figures/fig3.png)
<!-- Description: Two cylinders crossing at right angles under load P, with diameters D₂ for the upper cylinder and D₁ for the lower cylinder. -->

$$
c = \alpha \sqrt[3]{PK_DC_E}
$$

$$
d = \beta \sqrt[3]{PK_DC_E}
$$

$$
(\sigma_c)_{\max} = \frac{1.5P}{\pi cd}
$$

$$
y = \lambda \sqrt[3]{\frac{P^2C_E^2}{K_D}}
$$

$$
\tau_{\max} = \frac{1}{3}(\sigma_c)_{\max}
$$

$$
K_D = \frac{D_1D_2}{D_1 + D_2}
$$

and $\alpha$, $\beta$, and $\lambda$ depend upon $\dfrac{D_1}{D_2}$ as shown
