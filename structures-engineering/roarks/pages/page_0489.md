Case 8. Annular plate with, from $r_o$ to $a$, a uniform temperature differential $\Delta T$ between the bottom and the top surface (the midplane temperature is assumed to be unchanged, and so no in-plane forces develop)

![Figure 11.2: Annular plate with uniform temperature differential between bottom and top surface](figures/fig11_2.png)
<!-- Description: Schematic of an annular plate showing radius a, inner radius b, temperature differential from r_o to a, deflection y_b, slopes \theta_b and \theta_a, moments M_rb and M_ra, and shear Q_a. -->

General expressions for deformations, moments, and shears:

$$
y = y_b + \theta_b rF_1 + M_{rb}\frac{r^2}{D}F_2 + Q_b\frac{r^3}{D}F_3 + \frac{\gamma(1+\nu)\Delta T}{t}r^2G_2
$$

$$
\theta = \theta_bF_4 + M_{rb}\frac{r}{D}F_5 + Q_b\frac{r^2}{D}F_6 + \frac{\gamma(1+\nu)\Delta T}{t}rG_5
$$

$$
M_r = \theta_b\frac{D}{r}F_7 + M_{rb}F_8 + Q_brF_9 + \frac{\gamma(1+\nu)\Delta T}{t}D(G_8-\langle r-r_o\rangle^0)
$$

$$
M_t = \theta_b\frac{D(1-\nu^2)}{r}F_7 + \nu M_r - \frac{\gamma(1-\nu^2)\Delta TD}{t}\langle r-r_o\rangle^0
$$

$$
Q = Q_b\frac{b}{r}
$$

Note: If the temperature difference $\Delta T$ occurs over the entire plate, $r > r_o$, everywhere, so $\langle r-r_o\rangle^0 = 1$ everywhere, therefore, all numerical data for $K_{Mb}$ are given at a radius just greater than $b$.

For the numerical data given below, $\nu = 0.3$

$$
y = K_y\frac{\gamma \Delta T a^2}{t}, \qquad
\theta = K_\theta\frac{\gamma \Delta T a}{t}, \qquad
M = K_M\frac{\gamma \Delta T D}{t}, \qquad
Q = K_Q\frac{\gamma \Delta T D}{at}
$$
