## 7.3 Composite Members

A tension or compression member may be made up of parallel elements or parts which jointly carry the applied load. The essential problem is to determine how the load is apportioned among the several parts, and this is easily done by the method of consistent deformations. If the parts are so arranged that all undergo the same total elongation or shortening, then each will carry a portion of the load proportional to its stiffness, i.e., proportional to $AE/l$ if each is a uniform bar and proportional to $AE$ if all these uniform bars are of equal length. It follows that if there are $n$ bars, with section areas $A_1, A_2, \ldots, A_n$, lengths $l_1, l_2, \ldots, l_n$, and moduli $E_1, E_2, \ldots, E_n$, then the loads on the several bars $P_1, P_2, \ldots, P_n$ are given by

$$
P_1 = P \frac{\dfrac{A_1E_1}{l_1}}{\dfrac{A_1E_1}{l_1} + \dfrac{A_2E_2}{l_2} + \cdots + \dfrac{A_nE_n}{l_n}}
\tag{7.3-1}
$$

$$
P_2 = P \frac{\dfrac{A_2E_2}{l_2}}{\dfrac{A_1E_1}{l_1} + \dfrac{A_2E_2}{l_2} + \cdots + \dfrac{A_nE_n}{l_n}}
\tag{7.3-2}
$$
