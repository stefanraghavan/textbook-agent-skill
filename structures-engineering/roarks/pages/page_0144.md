## EXAMPLE

A bimetallic strip is made by bonding a piece of titanium alloy $\frac{1}{4}$ in wide by 0.030 in thick to a piece of stainless steel $\frac{1}{4}$ in wide by 0.060 in thick. For titanium, $E = 17 \cdot 10^6$ lb/in$^2$ and $\gamma = 5.7 \cdot 10^{-6}$ in/in/$^\circ$F; for stainless steel, $E = 28 \cdot 10^6$ lb/in$^2$ and $\gamma = 9.6 \cdot 10^{-6}$ in/in/$^\circ$F. It is desired to find the length of bimetal required to develop a reaction force of 5 oz at a simply supported left end when the right end is fixed and the temperature is raised $50^\circ$F; also the maximum stresses must be determined.

**Solution.** First find the value of $K_1$ from Eq. (8.2-2) and then evaluate the equivalent stiffness from Eq. (8.2-1):

$$
K_1 = 4 + 6\frac{0.03}{0.06} + 4\left(\frac{0.03}{0.06}\right)^2 + \frac{17}{28}\left(\frac{0.03}{0.06}\right)^3 + \frac{28\,0.06}{17\,0.03} = 11.37
$$

$$
\text{Equivalent } EI = \frac{0.25(0.06^3)(0.03)(28\cdot10^6)(17\cdot10^6)}{12[0.03(17\cdot10^6)+0.06(28\cdot10^6)]}\,11.37 = 333 \text{ lb-in}^2
$$

Under a temperature rise over the entire length, the bimetallic strip curves just as a single strip would curve under a temperature differential. To use case 6c in Table 8.1, the equivalent to $\gamma(T_2-T_1)/t$ must be found. This equivalent value is given by

$$
\frac{6(9.6\cdot10^{-6}-5.7\cdot10^{-6})(50)(0.03+0.06)}{(0.06^2)(11.37)} = 0.00257 \text{ in}^{-1}
$$

The expression for $R_A$ can now be obtained from case 6c in Table 8.1 and, noting that $a = 0$, the value of the length $l$ can be determined:

$$
R_A=\frac{-3(l^2-a^2)}{2l^3}EI\frac{\gamma}{t}(T_2-T_1)=\frac{-3}{2l}(333)(0.00257)=\frac{-5}{16}\text{ lb}
$$

Therefore $l = 4.11$ in.

The maximum bending moment is found at the fixed end and is equal to $R_A l$:

$$
\max M = -\frac{5}{16}(4.11) = -1.285 \text{ lb-in}
$$
