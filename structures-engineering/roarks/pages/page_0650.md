# TABLE 13.4 Formulas for discontinuity stresses and deformations at the junctions of shells and plates *(Continued)*

**2b. Axial load $P$**

![Figure 13.4: Axial load P at junction of cylinder and cone](figures/fig13_4.png)
<!-- Description: Sketch of a cylindrical shell joined to a conical shell, with axial tensile load P applied outward at both ends. -->

$$
LT_{A1}=\frac{-\nu_1 R_1^2}{2t_1^2}
$$

$$
LT_{A2}=\frac{\nu_2 R_1^2 E_1}{2E_2 t_1 t_2 \cos \alpha_2}+\frac{R_1 C_{AA2}\tan \alpha_2}{2t_1}
$$

$$
LT_{AC}=0
$$

$$
LT_{B1}=0
$$

$$
LT_{B2}=\frac{E_1 R_1^2 \tan \alpha_2}{2E_2 R_2 t_2 \cos \alpha_2}+\frac{R_1 C_{AB2}\tan \alpha_2}{2t_1}
$$

$$
LT_{BC}=0
$$

At the junction of the cylinder and cone,

$$
V_1=\frac{Pt_1K_{V1}}{\pi R_1^2}, \qquad
M_1=\frac{Pt_1^2K_{M1}}{\pi R_1^2}, \qquad
N_1=\frac{P}{2\pi R_1}
$$

$$
\Delta R_A=\frac{Pt_1}{E_1\pi R_1^2}\left(LT_{A1}-K_{V1}C_{AA1}-K_{M1}C_{AB1}\right)
$$

$$
\psi_A=\frac{P}{E_1\pi R_1^2}\left(K_{V1}C_{AB1}+K_{M1}C_{BB1}\right)
$$

For axial tension, $R_1=R_2$, $E_1=E_2$, $\nu_1=\nu_2=0.3$, and for $R/t>5$.

$$
\Delta R_A=\frac{P\nu_1}{2\pi E_1 t_1}K_{\Delta R_A}, \qquad
\psi_A=\frac{P\nu_1}{2\pi E_1 t_1^2}K_{\psi_A}, \qquad
\sigma_2=\frac{P}{2\pi R_1 t_1}K_{\sigma_2}
$$
