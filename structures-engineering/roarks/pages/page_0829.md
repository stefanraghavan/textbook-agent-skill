Composite members, as discussed in Secs. 8.3 and 8.8, are composed of more than one material where each material is of a continuous and homogeneous cross-section. The equivalent stiffnesses of these members are determined by a simple technique using an equivalent width. Composite materials, on the other hand, are made up of more than one material continuously dispersed at the macroscopic level at various angular orientations, and obtaining the combined material properties is much more complex. To this end, computer software is available. Once the material properties have been obtained from the software, the equations and tables provided in this book can be utilized.
