This is the main advantage that BEM has over FEM, which requires three-dimensional elements throughout the entire volumetric domain. Boundary elements for a general three-dimensional solid are quadrilateral or triangular surface elements covering the surface area of the component. For two-dimensional and axisymmetric problems, only line elements tracing the outline of the component are necessary.

Although BEM offers some modeling advantages over FEM, the latter can analyze more types of engineering applications and is much more firmly entrenched in today’s computer-aided-design (CAD) environment. Development of engineering applications of BEM are proceeding however, and more will be seen of the method in the future.
