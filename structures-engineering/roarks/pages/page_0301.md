For this case $M = Kpa^2$, where $M$ is the bending moment at a section a distance $x$ along the ring from the end of the minor axis, $p$ is the outward normal pressure per linear inch, and $K$ is a coefficient that depends on the ratios $b/a$ and $x/S$, where $S$ is one-quarter of the perimeter of the ring. Values of $M$ calculated by the preceding coefficients are correct only for a ring of uniform moment of inertia $I$; if $I$ is not uniform, then a correction $\Delta M$ must be added. This correction is given by

$$
\Delta M = -\frac{\int_0^x \frac{M}{I}\,dx}{\int_0^x \frac{dx}{I}}
$$

The integrals can be evaluated graphically. Reference 12 gives charts for the calculation of moments in elliptical rings under uniform radial loading; the preceding values of $K$ were taken from these charts.
