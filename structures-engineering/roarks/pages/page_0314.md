**10. Segment of a solid circle, base outward**

![Figure 10: Segment of a solid circle, base outward](figures/fig10.png)
<!-- Description: Diagram of a circular segment with base outward, showing dimensions and labels $R_x$, $R$, $r_i$, $c$, $c_1$, $d$, and angles $\alpha$. -->

*Note: $R$, $c$, $e/c$, $k_i$, and $k_o$ are all independent of the width of the segment provided all horizontal elements of the segment change width proportionately. To use these expressions for a segment of an ellipse, refer to the explanation in case 9.*

$$
R = R_x + c - a
$$

$$
\frac{a}{c} = \frac{3\alpha - 3\sin\alpha\cos\alpha}{3\alpha - 3\sin\alpha\cos\alpha - 2\sin^3\alpha}
\qquad
\frac{c_1}{c} = \frac{3\sin\alpha - 3\alpha\cos\alpha - \sin^3\alpha}{3\alpha - 3\sin\alpha\cos\alpha - 2\sin^3\alpha}
$$

$$
k_i = \frac{I}{Ac^2}\frac{1}{e/c}\frac{1-e/c}{R/c-1}
$$

where expressions for $I$ and $A$ are found in Table A.1, case 19

$$
k_o = \frac{I}{Ac^2}\frac{1}{(e/c)(c_1/c)}\frac{e/c + c_1/c}{R/c + c_1/c}
$$

$$
\int_{\text{area}} \frac{dA}{r}
= 2R_x\alpha + 2a\sin\alpha - 2\sqrt{R_x^2-a^2}\left(\frac{\pi}{2} + \sin^{-1}\frac{a-R_x\cos\alpha}{R_x-a\cos\alpha}\right)
$$

$$
\frac{e}{c} = \frac{R}{c} -
\frac{(\alpha-\sin\alpha\cos\alpha)a/c}
{
\frac{2\alpha R_x}{a} + 2\sin\alpha - 2\sqrt{\left(\frac{R_x}{a}\right)^2-1}
\left(
\frac{\pi}{2} + \sin^{-1}\frac{1-(R_x/a)\cos\alpha}{R_x/a-\cos\alpha}
\right)
}
$$

*(Note: Values of $\sin^{-1}$ between $-\pi/2$ and $\pi/2$ are to be taken in above expressions.)*

**11. Hollow circular section**

![Figure 11: Hollow circular section](figures/fig11.png)
<!-- Description: Diagram of a hollow circular section showing concentric circles and dimensions $R$, $c$, $c_1$, and $d$. -->

$$
\frac{e}{c} = \frac{1}{2}\left[\frac{2R}{c} - \sqrt{\left(\frac{R}{c}\right)^2-1} - \sqrt{\left(\frac{R}{c}\right)^2-\left(\frac{c_1}{c}\right)^2}\right]
$$

$$
k_i = \frac{1}{4e/c}\frac{1-e/c}{R/c-1}\left[1+\left(\frac{c_1}{c}\right)^2\right]
$$

$$
k_o = \frac{1}{4e/c}\frac{1+e/c}{R/c+1}\left[1+\left(\frac{c_1}{c}\right)^2\right]
$$

*(Note: For thin-walled tubes the discussion on page 277 should be considered)*
