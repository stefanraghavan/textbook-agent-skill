| 16. | V-notch in a circular shaft | 16c. Elastic stress, torsion |

![Figure 16: V-notch in a circular shaft](figures/fig16.png)
<!-- Description: A circular shaft with a V-notch is shown in side view and end view. Dimensions labeled include $D$, $h$, $r$, and notch angle $\theta$. -->

The stress concentration factor for the V-notch, $K_{tv}$, is the smaller of the values

$$
K_{tv} = K_{tu}
\qquad \text{or} \qquad
K_{tv} = 1.065K_{tu} - \left[0.022 + 0.137\left(\frac{\theta}{135}\right)^2\right](K_{tu} - 1)K_{tu}
$$

for $\dfrac{r}{D-2h} \leq 0.01$ and $\theta \leq 135^\circ$ where $K_{tu}$ is the stress concentration factor for a U-notch, case 15c, when the dimensions $h$, $r$, and $D$ are the same as for the V-notch and $\theta$ is the notch angle in degrees.

(Refs. 1 and 44)
