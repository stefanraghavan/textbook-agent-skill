**7. Ring under any number of equal radial forces equally spaced**

![Figure 9.2-7: Ring under any number of equal radial forces equally spaced](figures/fig9_2_7.png)
<!-- Description: Circular ring with several equally spaced outward radial forces W, points A and B marked near the top, and angles 2őł indicated between load positions. -->

For $0 < x < \theta$
$$
M=\frac{WR(u/s-k_2/\theta)}{2}
\qquad
N=\frac{Wu}{2s}
\qquad
V=\frac{-Wz}{2s}
$$

$$
\text{Max }+M=M_A=\frac{WR(1/s-k_2/\theta)}{2}
\qquad
\text{Max }-M=\frac{-WR}{2}\left(\frac{k_2}{\theta}-\frac{c}{s}\right)
\text{ at each load position}
$$

Radial displacement at each load point $=\Delta R_B$
$$
= \frac{WR^3}{EI}\left[\frac{k_1(\theta-sc)}{4s^2}+\frac{k_2c}{2s}-\frac{k_2^2}{2\theta}\right]
$$

Radial displacement at $x=0, 2\theta, \ldots = \Delta R_A$
$$
= \frac{-WR^3}{EI}\left[\frac{k_1(s-\theta c)}{4s^2}-\frac{k_2}{2s}+\frac{k_2^2}{2\theta}\right]
$$

If $\alpha=\beta=0$, $M=K_MWR$, $\Delta R=K_{\Delta R}WR^3/EI$

**8.**

![Figure 9.2-8: Circular ring with load over lower segment](figures/fig9_2_8.png)
<!-- Description: Circular ring with top point A and lower point C, symmetric angle őł shown from the vertical centerline, a distributed upward load w acting across the bottom, dimensions 2R and R sin őł indicated, and note that őł ‚Č• ŌÄ/2. -->

*(Note: $\theta \ge \frac{\pi}{2}$)*

$$
LT_M=-\frac{wR^2}{2}(z-s)^2\langle x-\theta\rangle^0
$$

$$
LT_N=-wRz(z-s)\langle x-\theta\rangle^0
$$

$$
LT_V=-wRu(z-s)\langle x-\theta\rangle^0
$$

$$
M_A=\frac{wR^2}{2\pi}\left[\pi(s^2-0.5)-\frac{sc-\theta}{2}-s^2\left(\theta+\frac{2s}{3}\right)-k_2(2s+sc-\pi+\theta)\right]
$$

$$
M_C=\frac{-wR^2}{2\pi}\left[\frac{\pi}{2}+\frac{sc}{2}-\frac{\theta}{2}+\theta s^2-\frac{2s^3}{3}+k_2(2s+sc-\pi+\theta)\right]
$$

$$
N_A=\frac{-wRs^3}{3\pi}
$$

$$
V_A=0
$$

$$
\Delta D_H=\frac{-wR^4}{2EI\pi}\left[k_1\frac{\pi s^3}{3}+k_2(\pi-2\pi s^2-\theta+2\theta s^2+sc)+2k_2^2(2s+sc-\pi+\theta)\right]
$$

$$
\Delta D_V=\frac{wR^4}{2EI\pi}\left[k_1\pi\left(\pi s-\theta s-\frac{2}{3}-c+\frac{c^3}{3}\right)-k_2(\pi c^2+sc-\theta+2\theta s^2)-2k_2^2(2s+sc-\pi+\theta)\right]
$$

$$
\Delta L=\frac{wR^4}{4EI\pi}\left[\pi(n-\theta)\frac{2s^2-1}{2}-\frac{\pi sc}{2}-2\pi k_1\left(\frac{2}{3}-\pi s+c+\theta s-\frac{c^3}{3}+\frac{s^3}{3\pi}\right)-k_2(sc+\pi-\theta+2\theta s^2+2s\pi-\pi^2+\pi\theta+\pi sc)-2k_2^2(2s+sc-\pi+\theta)\right]
$$

Max $+M$ occurs at an angular position $x_1$ where $x_1>\theta$, $x_1>123.1^\circ$, and
$$
\tan x_1+\frac{3\pi(s-\sin x_1)}{s^3}=0
$$

$$
\text{Max }-M=M_C
$$

If $\alpha=\beta=0$, $M=K_MwR^2$, $N=K_NwR$, $\Delta D=K_{\Delta D}wR^4/EI$, etc.
