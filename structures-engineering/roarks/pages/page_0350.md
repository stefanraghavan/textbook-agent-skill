# TABLE 9.3 Reaction and deformation formulas for circular arches *(Continued)*

**51. Partial second-order increase in distributed radial loading**

![Figure 9.3-51: Partial second-order increase in distributed radial loading](figures/fig9_3_51.png)
<!-- Description: Circular arch segment with distributed radial load $w$ over part of the span; angle $\phi$ indicated from the vertical reference line. -->

$$
LF_H=\frac{wR}{(\theta+\phi)^2}\left\{k_1[(\theta+\phi)(2c+m)-2s-2n-c^2n-scm]+\frac{k_2}{3}\left[3(\theta+\phi)(\theta s+\phi s+2c)-6s-6n-c(\theta+\phi)^3\right]\right\}
$$

$$
LF_V=\frac{wR}{(\theta+\phi)^2}\left\{k_1[(\theta+\phi)(2s-n)+mc^2-3m-scn+2c]-\frac{k_2}{3}\left[3(\theta+\phi)(\theta c+\phi c+2s)-6c-6m+12c(mc-sn)-s(\theta+\phi)^3\right]\right\}
$$

$$
LF_M=\frac{wR}{(\theta+\phi)^2}\left\{\frac{k_2}{3}[6(sm+cn-\theta-\phi)+(\theta+\phi)^3]\right\}
$$

If $\phi=\theta$ (the full span is loaded)

$$
LF_H=\frac{wR}{2\theta^2}\left[k_1(3\theta c-3s+s^3)+2k_2\left(\theta c-s+s\theta^2-\frac{2c\theta^3}{3}\right)\right]
$$

$$
LF_V=\frac{wR}{2\theta^2}\left[k_1s(\theta-sc)+2k_2\left(2s^2c-\theta s-c\theta^2+\frac{2s\theta^3}{3}\right)\right]
$$

$$
LF_M=\frac{wR}{\theta^2}\left[k_2\left(sc-\theta+\frac{2\theta^3}{3}\right)\right]
$$

**5m. Partial uniformly distributed tangential loading**

![Figure 9.3-5m: Partial uniformly distributed tangential loading](figures/fig9_3_5m.png)
<!-- Description: Circular arch segment with uniformly distributed tangential load $w$ acting along part of the arch; angle $\phi$ shown from the vertical centerline. -->

$$
LF_H=\frac{wR}{2}\left[(\theta+\phi)^2c+k_1(\theta n+\phi n-scn-s^2m+2m-2c)+2k_2(m-c-\theta s-\phi s)\right]
$$

$$
LF_V=\frac{wR}{2}\left[-(\theta+\phi)^2s+k_1(\theta m+\phi m+c^2n+scm-2s-2n)+2k_2(\theta c+\phi c+2s^2n-n-2scm+s)\right]
$$

$$
LF_M=\frac{wR}{2}\left[-(\theta+\phi)^2+2k_2(1+sn-cm)\right]
$$

If $\phi=\theta$ (the full span is loaded)

$$
LF_H=wR[2\theta^2c+k_1s(\theta-sc)-k_2\,2\theta s]
$$

$$
LF_V=wR[-2\theta^2s+k_1(\theta c-s-s^3)+2k_2(\theta c+s-2sc^2)]
$$

$$
LF_M=wR(-2\theta^2+k_2\,2s^2)
$$

For $\alpha=\beta=0$
