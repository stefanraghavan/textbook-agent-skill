# TABLE 13.4 Formulas for discontinuity stresses and deformations at the junctions of shells and plates *(Continued)*

**9c.** Hydrostatic internal* pressure $q_1$ at the junction where the angle to the position of zero pressure, $\theta > 3/\beta$.† If $\theta < 3/\beta$ the discontinuity in pressure gradient introduces small deformations at the junction.

![Figure 13.4: hydrostatic internal pressure at the junction of sphere and plate](figures/fig13_4.png)
<!-- Description: Sketch of a sphere joined to a plate at the right side, showing hydrostatic pressure q1 varying over the sphere, dimensions x1, R2, and annular line load w2 at the plate junction. -->

*Note:* There is no axial load on the junction. An axial load on the left end of the sphere balances any axial component of the pressure on the sphere, and the axial load on the plate is reacted by the annular line load $w_2 = q_1 b_1^2 \sin^2 \phi_1 /(2R_2)$ at a radius $R_2$.

$$
LT_{A1} = \frac{b_1^2 \sin \phi_1}{t_1^2}
$$

$$
LT_{A2} = \frac{E_1 t_2 b_1^2 \sin \phi_1}{32D_2 t_1 R_1} K_{P2}
\qquad \text{For } K_{P2} \text{ use the expressions from case 9a}
$$

For $LT_{AC}$ use the expression from case 9a

$$
LT_{B1} = -\frac{b_1 R_1 \sin \phi_1}{x_1 t_1}
$$

$$
LT_{B2} = \frac{E_1 b_1^2 \sin \phi_1}{16D_2 R_1} K_{P2}
$$

For $LT_{BC}$ use the expression from case 9a

At the junction of the sphere and plate,

$$
V_1 = q_1 t_1 K_{V1}, \qquad M_1 = q_1 t_1^2 K_{M1}
$$

$$
N_1 = -V_1 \cos \phi_1
$$

$$
\Delta R_A = \frac{q_1 t_1}{E_1}(LT_{A1} - K_{V1} C_{AA1} - K_{M1} C_{AB1})
$$

$$
\psi_A = \frac{q_1}{E_1}(-LT_{B1} + K_{V1} C_{AB1} + K_{M1} C_{BB1})
$$

For internal pressure, $E_1 = E_2$, $\nu_1 = \nu_2 = 0.3$, $x_1 = R_1$, $a_2 = a_1 \sin \phi_1$, $R_2 = 0.8a_2$, and for $R_1/t > 5$ and $R_A/t_2 > 4$. *(Note: No correction terms are used.)*

$$
\Delta R_A = \frac{q_1 R_1^2}{E_1 t_1} K_{\Delta R_A},
\qquad
\psi_A = \frac{q_1 R_1}{E_1 t_1} K_{\psi A},
\qquad
\sigma_2 = \frac{q_1 R_1}{t_1} K_{\sigma 2}
$$
