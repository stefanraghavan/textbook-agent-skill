# TABLE 13.4 Formulas for discontinuity stresses and deformations at the junctions of shells and plates *(Continued)*

**6b. Axial load $P$**

![Figure 13.4: Axial load P at the junction of a cone and sphere](figures/fig13_4.png)
<!-- Description: A cone joined to a spherical shell, with axial load P applied in tension along the axis at both ends. -->

$$
LT_{A1}=\frac{-\nu_1R_1^2}{2t_1^2\cos\alpha_1}
$$

$$
LT_{A2}=\frac{R_1^2E_1(1+\nu_2)}{2E_2t_1t_2\sin\phi_2}+\frac{R_1C_{AA2}}{2t_1}\left(\tan\alpha_1+\frac{1}{\tan\phi_2}\right)
$$

$$
LT_{AC}=0
$$

$$
LT_{B1}=\frac{-R_1\tan\alpha_1}{2t_1\cos\alpha_1}
$$

$$
LT_{BC}=0
$$

$$
LT_{B2}=\frac{R_1C_{AB2}}{2t_1}\left(\tan\alpha_1+\frac{1}{\tan\phi_2}\right)
$$

For axial tension, $E_1=E_2,\ \nu_1=\nu_2=0.3,\ t_1=t_2,\ R_2\sin\phi_2=R_1$, and for $R_1/t_1\cos\alpha_1>5$ and $R_2/t_2>5$.

$$
\Delta R_A=\frac{P\nu_1}{2\pi E_1t_1}K_{\Delta RA};\qquad
\psi_A=\frac{P\nu_1}{2\pi E_1t_1^2}K_{\psi A};\qquad
\sigma_2=\frac{P}{2\pi R_1t_1}K_{\sigma 2}
$$

At the junction of the cone and sphere,

$$
V_1=\frac{Pt_1K_{V1}}{\pi R_1^2},\qquad
M_1=\frac{Pt_1^2K_{M1}}{\pi R_1^2}
$$

$$
N_1=\frac{P}{2\pi R_1\cos\alpha_1}-V_1\sin\alpha_1
$$

$$
\Delta R_A=\frac{Pt_1}{E_1\pi R_1^2}\left(LT_{A1}-K_{V1}C_{AA1}-K_{M1}C_{AB1}\right)
$$

$$
\psi_A=\frac{P}{E_1\pi R_1^2}\left(-LT_{B1}+K_{V1}C_{AB1}+K_{M1}C_{BB1}\right)
$$
