# EXAMPLE

The state of stress at a point relative to an $xyz$ coordinate system is given by the stress matrix

$$
[\sigma]_{xyz} =
\begin{bmatrix}
-8 & 6 & -2 \\
6 & 4 & 2 \\
-2 & 2 & -5
\end{bmatrix}
\text{ MPa}
$$

Determine the state of stress on an element that is oriented by first rotating the $xyz$ axes $45^\circ$ about the $z$ axis, and then rotating the resulting axes $30^\circ$ about the new $x$ axis.

**Solution.** The surface normals can be found by a series of coordinate transformations for each rotation. From Fig. 2.10(a), the vector components for the first rotation can be represented by

$$
\left\{
\begin{array}{c}
x_1 \\
y_1 \\
z_1
\end{array}
\right\}
=
\begin{bmatrix}
\cos \theta & \sin \theta & 0 \\
-\sin \theta & \cos \theta & 0 \\
0 & 0 & 1
\end{bmatrix}
\left\{
\begin{array}{c}
x \\
y \\
z
\end{array}
\right\}
\qquad (a)
$$

The last rotation establishes the $x'y'z'$ coordinates as shown in Fig. 2.10(b), and they are related to the $x_1 y_1 z_1$ coordinates by

$$
\left\{
\begin{array}{c}
x' \\
y' \\
z'
\end{array}
\right\}
=
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 0 & 0 \\
0 & \cos \varphi & \sin \varphi \\
0 & -\sin \varphi & \cos \varphi
\end{bmatrix}
\left\{
\begin{array}{c}
x_1 \\
y_1 \\
z_1
\end{array}
\right\}
\qquad (b)
$$

Substituting Eq. $(a)$ in $(b)$ gives

$$
\left\{
\begin{array}{c}
x' \\
y' \\
z'
\end{array}
\right\}
=
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 0 & 0 \\
0 & \cos \varphi & \sin \varphi \\
0 & -\sin \varphi & \cos \varphi
\end{bmatrix}
\begin{bmatrix}
\cos \theta & \sin \theta & 0 \\
-\sin \theta & \cos \theta & 0 \\
0 & 0 & 1
\end{bmatrix}
\left\{
\begin{array}{c}
x \\
y \\
z
\end{array}
\right\}
$$

$$
=
\begin{bmatrix}
\cos \theta & \sin \theta & 0 \\
-\sin \theta \cos \varphi & \cos \theta \cos \varphi & \sin \varphi \\
\sin \theta \sin \varphi & -\cos \theta \sin \varphi & \cos \varphi
\end{bmatrix}
\left\{
\begin{array}{c}
x \\
y \\
z
\end{array}
\right\}
\qquad (c)
$$
