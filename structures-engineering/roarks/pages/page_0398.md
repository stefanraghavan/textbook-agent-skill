The second portion of the solution considers a beam fixed at the right end against both rotation and warping and free at the left end. It is loaded at a point 4 ft from the left end with an unknown torque $T_c$, which makes the angle of rotation zero at that point, and a known torque of $300(20) + 200(60) = 18{,}000 \text{ lb-in}$ at a point 12 ft from the left end. Again evaluate the following constants; assume $G = 12(10^6) \text{ lb/in}^2$.

$$
K = \frac{1}{3}(4 + 8 + 10)(0.3^3) = 0.198 \text{ in}^4 \qquad \text{(Table 10.2, case 7)}
$$

$$
C_w = \frac{(10^2)(0.3)(4^3)(8^3)}{(12)(4^3 + 8^3)} = 142.2 \text{ in}^6 \qquad \text{(Table 10.2, case 7)}
$$

$$
\beta = \left( \frac{KG}{C_w E} \right)^{1/2}
= \left[ \frac{(0.198)(12)(10^6)}{(142.2)(30)(10^6)} \right]^{1/2}
= 0.0236 \text{ in}^{-1}
$$

Therefore $\beta l = 0.0236(20)(12) = 5.664,\ \beta(l - a) = 0.0236(20 - 12)(12) = 2.2656$ for $a = 12 \text{ ft}$, and $\beta(l - a) = 0.0236(20 - 4)(12) = 4.5312$ for $a = 4 \text{ ft}$.

From Table 10.3, case 1b, consider two torsional loads: an unknown torque $T_c$ at $a = 4 \text{ ft}$ and a torque of $18{,}000 \text{ lb-in}$ at $a = 12 \text{ ft}$. The following constants are needed:

$$
C_1 = \cosh \beta l = \cosh 5.664 = 144.1515
$$

$$
C_2 = \sinh \beta l = \sinh 5.664 = 144.1480
$$

For $a = 4 \text{ ft}$,

$$
C_{a3} = \cosh \beta(l - a) - 1 = \cosh 4.5312 - 1 = 45.4404
$$

$$
C_{a4} = \sinh \beta(l - a) - \beta(l - a) = \sinh 4.5312 - 4.5312 = 41.8984
$$

For $a = 12 \text{ ft}$,

$$
C_{a3} = \cosh 2.2656 - 1 = 3.8703
$$

$$
C_{a4} = \sinh 2.2656 - 2.2656 = 2.5010
