# TABLE 8.8 Shear, moment, slope, and deflection formulas for beams under simultaneous axial compression and transverse loading *(Continued)*

SEC. 8.17

## 5b. Left end guided, right end fixed

![Figure 5b](figures/fig5b.png)
<!-- Description: Beam with left end guided and right end fixed, axial load P at the left, transverse deflection $\Delta_o$ near the right, and distance $a$. -->

$$
R_A = 0 \qquad \theta_A = 0
$$

$$
M_A = \Delta_o P \frac{\sin k(l-a)}{\sin kl}
$$

$$
y_A = -\Delta_o \frac{\sin k(l-a) + \sin ka}{\sin kl}
$$

$$
R_B = 0 \qquad \theta_B = 0 \qquad y_B = 0 \qquad M_B = -\Delta_o P \frac{\sin ka}{\sin kl}
$$

Max $+\,M = M_A$; max possible value $= \Delta_o P$ when $a = 0$

Max $-\,M = M_B$; max possible value $= -\Delta_o P$ when $a = l$

Max $y = y_A$; max possible value $= \dfrac{-\Delta_o}{\cos(kl/2)}$ when $a = \dfrac{l}{2}$

## 5c. Left end simply supported, right end fixed

![Figure 5c](figures/fig5c.png)
<!-- Description: Beam with left end simply supported and right end fixed, axial load P at the left, transverse deflection $\Delta_o$ near the right, and distance $a$. -->

$$
M_A = 0 \qquad y_A = 0
$$

$$
R_A = \Delta_o Pk \frac{\cos ka}{\sin kl - kl \cos kl}
$$

$$
\theta_A = -\Delta_o k \frac{C_3 C_{a1} + C_4 C_{a2}}{C_2 C_3 - C_1 C_4}
$$

$$
R_B = -R_A \qquad \theta_B = 0 \qquad y_B = 0 \qquad M_B = R_A l
$$

## 5d. Left end fixed, right end fixed

![Figure 5d](figures/fig5d.png)
<!-- Description: Beam with both ends fixed, axial load P at the left, transverse deflection $\Delta_o$ near the right, and distance $a$. -->

$$
\theta_A = 0 \qquad y_A = 0
$$

$$
R_A = \Delta_o Pk \frac{C_3 C_{a2} + C_2 C_{a1}}{C_3^2 - C_2 C_4}
$$

$$
M_A = -\Delta_o P \frac{C_3 C_{a1} + C_4 C_{a2}}{C_3^2 - C_2 C_4}
$$

$$
R_B = -R_A \qquad \theta_B = 0 \qquad y_B = 0 \qquad M_B = M_A + R_A l
$$

## 5e. Left end simply supported, right end simply supported

![Figure 5e](figures/fig5e.png)
<!-- Description: Beam with both ends simply supported, axial load P at the left, transverse deflection $\Delta_o$ near the right, and distance $a$. -->

$$
R_A = 0 \qquad M_A = 0 \qquad y_A = 0
$$

$$
\theta_A = -\Delta_o k \frac{\cos k(l-a)}{\sin kl}
$$

$$
R_B = 0 \qquad M_B = 0 \qquad y_B = 0
$$

$$
\theta_B = -\Delta_o k \frac{\cos ka}{\sin kl}
$$

Max $+\,M = \Delta_o P \dfrac{\sin ka}{\sin kl} \cos k(l-a)$ at $x$ just left of $a$; max possible value $= \Delta_o P$ when $a = l$

Max $-\,M = -\Delta_o P \dfrac{\cos ka}{\sin kl} \sin k(l-a)$ at $x$ just right of $a$; max possible value $= -\Delta_o P$ when $a = 0$

Max $+\,y = \Delta_o \dfrac{\cos ka}{\sin kl} \sin k(l-a)$ at $x$ just right of $a$; max possible value $= \Delta_o$ when $a = 0$

Max $-\,y = -\Delta_o \dfrac{\sin ka}{\sin kl} \cos k(l-a)$ at $x$ just left of $a$; max possible value $= -\Delta_o$ when $a = l$
