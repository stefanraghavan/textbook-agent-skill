# TABLE 13.4 Formulas for discontinuity stresses and deformations at the junctions of shells and plates *(Continued)*

5. Conical shell connected to another conical shell.* To ensure accuracy, for each cone evaluate $k_A$ and the value of $k$ in that cone at the position where $\mu = 4$. The absolute values of $k$ at all four positions should be greater than 5. $R/(t \cos \alpha)$ should also be greater than 5 at all these positions. $E_1$ and $E_2$ are the moduli of elasticity and $\nu_1$ and $\nu_2$ the Poisson ratios for the left and right cones, respectively. $b_1 = R_1 - (t_1 \cos^2 \alpha_1)/2,\ a_1 = R_1 + (t_1 \cos \alpha_1)/2$. $R_A = R_1$. Similar expressions are used for the right-hand cone. See Table 13.3, case 4, for formulas for $k_A,\ \beta,\ \mu,\ C_1$, and the $F$ functions for each of the two cones. Normally $R_2 = R_1$, but if $\alpha_1 + \alpha_2$ is close to zero the midthickness radii may not be equal at the junction. Under this condition a different set of correction terms will be used if they are necessary. Note that rather than use an additional level of subscripting in the following equations, use has been made of subscripted parentheses or brackets to denote which cone the coefficients refer to.

$$
K_{V1} = \frac{LT_A C_{BB} - LT_B C_{AB}}{C_{AA} C_{BB} - C_{AB}^2},
\qquad
K_{M1} = \frac{LT_B C_{AA} - LT_A C_{AB}}{C_{AA} C_{BB} - C_{AB}^2},
\qquad
\begin{aligned}
LT_A &= LT_{A1} + LT_{A2} + LT_{AC} \\
LT_B &= LT_{B1} + LT_{B2} + LT_{BC}
\end{aligned}
\qquad
\text{See cases 5a--5d for these load terms}
$$

$$
C_{AA} = C_{AA1} + C_{AA2},
\qquad
C_{AA1} = \frac{R_1 \sin \alpha}{t_1 \sqrt{2}}
\left[
\frac{k_A}{C_1}
\left(
F_{4A} - \frac{4\nu_1^2}{k_A^2} F_{2A}
\right)
\right]_1,
\qquad
C_{AA2} = \frac{R_1 E_1 \sin \alpha_2}{E_2 t_2 \sqrt{2}}
\left[
\frac{k_A}{C_1}
\left(
F_{4A} - \frac{4\nu_2^2}{k_A^2} F_{2A}
\right)
\right]_2
$$

$$
C_{AB} = C_{AB1} + C_{AB2},
\qquad
C_{AB1} = -\frac{R_1}{t_1}\left(\frac{\beta F_{7A}}{C_1}\right)_1,
\qquad
C_{AB2} = \frac{R_1 E_1 t_1}{E_2 t_2^2}\left(\frac{\beta F_{7A}}{C_1}\right)_2
$$

$$
C_{BB} = C_{BB1} + C_{BB2},
\qquad
C_{BB1} = \frac{R_1^2 \sqrt{2}}{t_1 \sin \alpha_1}
\left(
\frac{\beta^2 F_{2A}}{k_A C_1}
\right)_1,
\qquad
C_{BB2} = \frac{R_1 E_1 t_1^2 \sqrt{2}}{E_2 t_2^3 \sin \alpha_2}
\left(
\frac{\beta^2 F_{2A}}{k_A C_1}
\right)_2
$$

The stresses in the left-hand cone at the junction are given by

$$
\sigma_1 = \frac{N_1}{t_1},
\qquad
\sigma_2 = \frac{\Delta R_A E_1}{R_A} + \nu_1 \sigma_1
$$

$$
\sigma_1' = \frac{-6M_1}{t_1^2}
$$

$$
\sigma_2' =
\frac{-6V_1 \sin \alpha_1}{t_1 \beta_1}
\div
(1-\nu_1^2)
\left(\frac{F_{7A}}{C_1}\right)_1
+
\sigma_1'
\left[
\nu_1 + \frac{2\sqrt{2}}{k_A C_1}(1-\nu_1^2)F_{2A}
\right]_1
$$

\* Note: If either conical shell increases in radius away from the junction, substitute $-\alpha$ for $\alpha$ for that cone in all the appropriate formulas above and in those used from case 4, Table 13.3.
