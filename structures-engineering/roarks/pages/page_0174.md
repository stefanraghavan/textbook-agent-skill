## 8.11 Beams of Relatively Great Width

Because of prevention of the lateral deformation that would normally accompany the fiber stresses, wide beams, such as thin metallic strips, are more rigid than the formulas of Sec. 8.1 indicate. This stiffening effect is taken into account by using $E/(1 - \nu^2)$ instead of $E$ in the formulas for deflection and curvature if the beams are very wide (Ref. 21).
