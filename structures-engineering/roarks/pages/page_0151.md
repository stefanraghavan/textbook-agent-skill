# Formulas for Stress and Strain

## 8.4.

Substitute the following material properties and physical dimensions into case 1h: $l_1 = -100\ \text{mm}$, $l_2 = 150\ \text{mm}$, $l_3 = 75\ \text{mm}$, $a = -(100 - 40) = -60\ \text{mm}$, $E_1 I_1 = -7(10^6)\ \text{N-mm}^2$, and $E_2 I_2 = E_3 I_3 = 7(10^6)\ \text{N-mm}^2$. Using these data gives the frame and load constants as

$$
A_{HH} = 0.2708\ \text{in/lb}, \qquad A_{HM} = A_{MH} = -0.0008036\ \text{lb}^{-1}
$$

$$
A_{MM} = 0.00001786\ (\text{lb-in})^{-1}, \qquad LP_H = 0.0005464(2000) = 1.0928\ \text{in}
$$

$$
LP_M = -0.000009286(2000) = -0.01857\ \text{rad}
$$

Using these frames and load terms in case 1, the pinned-end case, the reaction and deformations are found to be

$$
\delta_{HA} = 0 \qquad M_A = 0 \qquad H_A = \frac{LP_H}{A_{HH}} = 4.0352\ \text{N} \qquad \text{(to left)}
$$

and

$$
\psi_A = A_{MH} H_A - LP_M = 0.01533\ \text{rad} \qquad \text{(clockwise)}
$$

Applying the three independent statics equations to Fig. 8.10(a) results in

$$
V_A = -13.214\ \text{N} \qquad H_B = -4.0358\ \text{N} \qquad \text{and} \qquad V_B = 13.214\ \text{N}
$$

Now treat each of the three members as separate bodies in equilibrium, and find the deformations as pinned-end beams using equations from Table 8.1 as appropriate.

For member 1 as shown in Fig. 8.10(b): Using case 3e twice, once for each of the two moment loadings, gives

$$
\theta_A =
\frac{-2000}{6(7 \cdot 10^6)(100)}[2(100)^2 - 6(60)(100) + 3(60)^2]
$$

$$
\qquad\qquad
+ \frac{1596.4}{6(7 \cdot 10^6)(100)}[2(100)^2 - 6(100)(100) + 3(100)^2]
$$

$$
= -0.001325\ \text{rad}
$$

$$
\theta_C =
\frac{2000}{6(7 \cdot 10^6)(100)}[(100)^2 - 3(60)^2]
- \frac{1596.4}{6(7 \cdot 10^6)(100)}[(100)^2 - 3(100)^2]
$$

$$
= 0.007221\ \text{rad}
$$

To obtain the angle $\psi_A = 0.01533\ \text{rad}$ at position $A$, member 1 must be given an additional rigid-body clockwise rotation of $0.01533 - 0.001325 = 0.01401\ \text{rad}$. This rigid-body motion moves position $C$ to the left a distance of $1.401\ \text{mm}$ and makes the slope at position $C$ equal to $0.007221 - 0.01401 = -0.006784\ \text{rad}$ (clockwise).

For member 3 as shown in Fig. 8.10(c): Again use case 3e from Table 8.1 twice to get

$$
\theta_C =
\frac{-1596.4}{6(7 \cdot 10^6)(75)}[2(75)^2 - 0 + 0]
- \frac{-605.35}{6(7 \cdot 10^6)(75)}[2(75)^2 - 6(75)(75) + 3(75)^2]
$$

$$
= -0.006782\ \text{rad}
$$

$$
\theta_D =
\frac{1596.4}{6(7 \cdot 10^6)(75)}[(75)^2 - 0]
+ \frac{-605.35}{6(7 \cdot 10^6)(75)}[(75)^2 - 3(75)^2]
$$

$$
= 0.005013\ \text{rad}
$$
