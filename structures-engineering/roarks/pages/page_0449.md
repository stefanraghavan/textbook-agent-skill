The formulas for stress may give either positive or negative results, depending upon $\delta$; a negative result indicates compressive stress, and a positive result a tensile stress. It is to be noted that $P$ also may become negative.

Wempner (Refs. 67 and 68) derives more exacting expressions for the conical spring. Unless the center hole is small or the cone angle is outside the range normally used for disk springs, however, the differences are slight. Reference 69 presents useful design curves based on Ref. 27.

Conical spring washers can be stacked to operate in either series or parallel. One must be careful to consider the effect of friction, however, when using them in the parallel configuration.
