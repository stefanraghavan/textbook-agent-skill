## 16. Unit axial segment of pipe filled with liquid of weight per unit volume $\rho$ and supported at the base

![Figure 16: Unit axial segment of pipe filled with liquid and supported at the base](figures/fig16.png)
<!-- Description: Circular pipe cross section filled with liquid, labeled A at the top and C at the bottom support, with upward reaction at C and distributed liquid shown by horizontal marks. -->

$$LT_M=\rho R^3\left(1-u-\frac{xz}{2}\right)$$

$$LT_N=\rho R^2\left(1-u-\frac{xz}{2}\right)$$

$$LT_V=\rho R^2\left(\frac{z}{2}-\frac{xu}{2}\right)$$

$$M_A=\rho R^3\left(0.75-\frac{k_2}{2}\right)$$

$$M_C=\rho R^3\left(1.25-\frac{k_2}{2}\right)$$

$$N_A=0.75\rho R^2$$

$$V_A=0$$

$$\Delta D_H=\frac{\rho R^5\,12(1-\nu^2)}{Et^3}\left[\frac{k_1\pi}{4}+k_2\left(2-\frac{\pi}{2}\right)-k_2^2\right]$$

$$\Delta D_V=\frac{-\rho R^5\,12(1-\nu^2)}{Et^3}\left(\frac{k_1\pi^2}{8}-2k_2+k_2^2\right)$$

$$\Delta L=\frac{-\rho R^5\,12(1-\nu^2)}{Et^3}\left[\frac{k_1 3\pi^2}{32}-k_2\left(0.5+\frac{\pi}{4}\right)+\frac{k_2^2}{2}\right]$$

*Note:* For this case and case 17,

$$\alpha=\frac{t^2}{12R^2(1-\nu^2)}$$

$$\beta=\frac{t^2}{6R^2(1-\nu)} \quad \text{where } t=\text{ pipe wall thickness}$$

$$\text{Max } + M=M_C$$

$$\text{Max } - M=-\rho R^3\left(\frac{k_2}{2}-0.1796\right)\qquad \text{at } x=105.23^\circ$$

If $\alpha=\beta=0$,

$$\Delta D_H=0.2146\frac{\rho R^5\,12(1-\nu^2)}{Et^3}$$

$$\Delta D_V=-0.2337\frac{\rho R^5\,12(1-\nu^2)}{Et^3}$$

$$\Delta L=-0.1399\frac{\rho R^5\,12(1-\nu^2)}{Et^3}$$

$$\rho R^2(\pi-\theta+sc)$$

*Note:* see case 16 for expressions for $\alpha$ and $\beta$

$$M_A=\frac{\rho R^3}{4\pi}\left[2\theta s^2+3sc-3\theta+\pi+2\pi c^2+2k_2[sc-2s+(\pi-\theta)(1-2c)]\right]$$

$$N_A=\frac{\rho R^2}{4\pi}\left[3sc+(\pi-\theta)(1+2c^2)\right]$$

$$V_A=0$$

$$\Delta D_H=
\begin{cases}
\displaystyle \frac{\rho R^5 3(1-\nu^2)}{2Et^3\pi}\left\{k_1\pi(sc+2\pi-3\theta+2\theta c^2)+8k_2\pi\left(2c-sc-\frac{\pi}{2}+\theta\right)+8k_2^2[(\pi-\theta)(1-2c)+sc-2s]\right\} & \text{for } \theta\le\frac{\pi}{2} \\[1.2em]
\displaystyle \frac{\rho R^5 3(1-\nu^2)}{2Et^3\pi}\left\{k_1\pi[(\pi-\theta)(1+2c^2)+3sc]+8k_2^2[(\pi-\theta)(1-2c)+sc-2s]\right\} & \text{for } \theta\ge\frac{\pi}{2}
\end{cases}
$$
