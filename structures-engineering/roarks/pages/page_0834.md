A general laminate can be subjected to both membrane (in-plane) and bending loads. CLPT assumes that the resulting strains and curvatures are uniform throughout the laminate. Because the plies are oriented in various directions, though, stresses are continuous only within the individual plies. Figure C.5 shows the variation in strain, modulus, and stress for a laminate subjected to a bending load. The strain variation is linear, as with any isotropic material. Because the ply moduli are discontinuous, however, ply stresses are also discontinuous. Stresses vary linearly within each ply, but are discontinuous at the ply boundaries.

Because the stresses are discontinuous, it is easier to define applied loads in terms of averaged stresses, or stress resultants. An element of a laminate can have up to six applied stress resultants, as shown in Figure C.6: three in-plane resultants $N_i$, and three bending resultants $M_i$.
