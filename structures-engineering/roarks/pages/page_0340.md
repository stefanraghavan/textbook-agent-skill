# TABLE 9.3 Reaction and deformation formulas for circular arches *(Continued)*

**1d. Concentrated tangential load**

![Figure 1d: Concentrated tangential load](figures/fig1d.png)
<!-- Description: A circular arch with a concentrated tangential load W applied near the left side; angle phi is indicated between dashed and vertical reference lines. -->

$$
LP_H = W\left[\theta c(cm-sn)+\phi c+\frac{k_1}{2}(0m+\phi m-scm-c^2n)-k_2c(sm+cn)\right]
$$

$$
LP_M = \frac{W}{2}\left[\theta(sn-cm)-\phi+\frac{k_1}{2s^2}(0cm-\theta+\phi sn-sc+sm)+k_2(sm+cn)\right]
$$

For $\alpha=\beta=0$

| $\theta$ | 30° | 60° | 90° |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| $\phi$ | 0° 15° | 0° 30° | 0° 45° |
| $\dfrac{LP_H}{W}$ | 0.0050 0.0081 | 0.1359 0.1920 | 0.7854 0.8330 |
| $\dfrac{LP_M}{W}$ | 0.0201 0.0358 | 0.1410 0.2215 | 0.3573 0.4391 |

**1e. Uniform vertical load on partial span**

![Figure 1e: Uniform vertical load on partial span](figures/fig1e.png)
<!-- Description: A circular arch with a uniformly distributed vertical load w over part of the span near the crown; angle phi is shown with dashed and vertical reference lines. -->

$$
LP_H=\frac{wR}{4}\left[\theta c(1+4sn+2s^2)+\phi c(m^2-n^2)-c(sc+mn)\right.
$$

$$
\left.+\frac{2k_1}{3}(n^3-3ns^2-2s^3)+2k_2c(2cn+cs-\theta-\phi-mn)\right]
$$

$$
LP_M=\frac{wR}{8}\left\{mn+sc-\theta(4sn+2s^2+1)-\phi(m^2-n^2)\right.
$$

$$
+\frac{k_1}{s}\left[\frac{\theta}{s}(n^2+s^2)+2(c-m)-\frac{2}{3}(c^3-m^3)+c(n^2-s^2)-2\phi n\right]
$$

$$
\left.+2k_2(\theta+\phi+mn-sc-2cn)\right\}
$$

If $\phi=0$ (the full span is loaded)

$$
LP_H=\frac{wR}{6}\left[3c(2\theta s^2+\theta-sc)-4k_1s^3+6k_2c(sc-\theta)\right]
$$

$$
LP_M=\frac{wR}{4}\left[sc-\theta-2\theta s^2+2k_2(\theta-sc)\right]
$$

For $\alpha=\beta=0$

| $\theta$ | 30° | 60° | 90° |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| $\phi$ | 0° 15° | 0° 30° | 0° 45° |
| $\dfrac{LP_H}{wR}$ | -0.0046 -0.0079 | -0.0969 -0.1724 | -0.3333 -0.6280 |
| $\dfrac{LP_M}{wR}$ | -0.0187 -0.0350 | -0.1029 -0.2031 | -0.1667 -0.3595 |

---

**1f. Uniform horizontal load on left side only**

![Figure 1f: Uniform horizontal load on left side only](figures/fig1f.png)
<!-- Description: A circular arch with a uniformly distributed horizontal load w acting on the left side only. -->

$$
LP_H=\frac{wR}{12}\left[30c(1-6c^2+4c)+3sc^2+k_1(60-6sc-120c+12c-8s^3)\right.
$$

$$
\left.+6k_2c(3sc-2s-\theta)\right]
$$

$$
LP_M=\frac{wR}{8}\left\{60c^2-\theta-40c-sc+\frac{k_1}{3s^2}[s(2-3c+c^3)-30(1-c)^2]\right.
$$

$$
\left.+2k_2(\theta+2s-3sc)\right\}
$$

For $\alpha=\beta=0$

| $\theta$ | 30° | 60° | 90° |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| $\dfrac{LP_H}{wR}$ | 0.0010 | 0.0969 | 1.1187 |
| $\dfrac{LP_M}{wR}$ | 0.0040 | 0.1060 | 0.5833 |
