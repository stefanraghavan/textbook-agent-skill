| **14.** Any thin tube of uniform thickness;  
$U =$ length of median boundary;  
$A =$ mean of areas enclosed by outer and inner boundaries, or (approximate) area within median boundary

![Figure 10.1-14: Thin tube of uniform thickness](figures/fig10_1_14.png)
<!-- Description: An oblong closed thin-walled tube cross section with uniform thickness $t$, indicated by an arrow, and a dashed median boundary. --> | $K=\dfrac{4A^2 t}{U}$ | $\tau_{\text{average}}=\dfrac{T}{2tA}$  (stress is nearly uniform if $t$ is small) |
| **15.** Any thin tube. $U$ and $A$ as for case 14; $t =$ thickness at any point

![Figure 10.1-15: Thin tube with varying thickness](figures/fig10_1_15.png)
<!-- Description: An oblong closed thin-walled tube cross section with varying thickness, with points $A$ and $B$ marked across a wall thickness segment $AB$, and local thickness $t$ indicated. --> | $K=\dfrac{4A^2}{\int dU/t}$ | $\tau_{\text{average}}$ on any thickness $AB=\dfrac{T}{2tA}$  $(\tau_{\max}\ \text{where}\ t\ \text{is a minimum})$ |
| **16.** Hollow rectangle, thin-walled

![Figure 10.1-16: Hollow rectangle, thin-walled](figures/fig10_1_16.png)
<!-- Description: A thin-walled rectangular hollow section with outer dimensions $a$ and $b$, wall thicknesses $t$ on the vertical sides and $t_1$ on the horizontal sides. -->  

*(Note: For thick-walled hollow rectangles see Refs. 16 and 25. Reference 25 illustrates how to extend the work presented to cases with more than one enclosed region.)* | $K=\dfrac{2tt_1(a-t)^2(b-t_1)^2}{at+bt_1-t^2-t_1^2}$ | $\tau_{\text{average}}=\begin{cases}
\dfrac{T}{2t(a-t)(b-t_1)} & \text{near midlength of short sides} \\
\dfrac{T}{2t_1(a-t)(b-t_1)} & \text{near midlength of long sides}
\end{cases}$

(There will be higher stresses at inner corners unless fillets of fairly large radius are provided) |
