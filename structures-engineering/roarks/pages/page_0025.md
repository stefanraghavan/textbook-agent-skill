## Shear Strain.

The change in shape of the element caused by the shear stresses can be first illustrated by examining the effect of $\tau_{xy}$ alone as shown in Fig. 2.7. The engineering shear strain $\gamma_{xy}$ is a measure of the skewing of the stressed element from a rectangular parallelepiped. In Fig. 2.7(b), the shear strain is defined as the change in the angle $BAD$. That is,

$$
\gamma_{xy}=\angle BAD-\angle B'A'D'
$$

where $\gamma_{xy}$ is in dimensionless radians.

For a linear, homogeneous, isotropic material, the shear strains in the $xy$, $yz$, and $zx$ planes are directly related to the shear stresses by

$$
\gamma_{xy}=\frac{\tau_{xy}}{G}
\qquad (2.2\text{-}6a)
$$

$$
\gamma_{yz}=\frac{\tau_{yz}}{G}
\qquad (2.2\text{-}6b)
$$

$$
\gamma_{zx}=\frac{\tau_{zx}}{G}
\qquad (2.2\text{-}6c)
$$

where the material constant, $G$, is called the *shear modulus*.
