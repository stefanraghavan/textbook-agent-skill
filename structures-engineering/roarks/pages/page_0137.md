Using the step function described previously, these equations can be reduced to

$$
V = 900 - 12x + 1500(x - 160)^0
$$

$$
M = 900x - 6x^2 + 1500(x - 160)
$$

These equations are plotted, giving the shear and moment diagrams shown in Fig. 8.2. The maximum positive moment evidently occurs between the supports; the exact location is found by setting the first shear equation equal to zero and solving for $x$, which gives $x = 75$ in. Substitution of this value of $x$ in the first moment equation gives $M = 33,750$ lb-in. The maximum negative moment occurs at the right support where the shear diagram again passes through zero and is 9600 lb-in.

The results obtained so far are independent of the cross section of the beam. The stresses will now be calculated for each of the sections $(a)$, $(b)$, and $(c)$.

$(a)$ For the rectangular section: $I = \frac{1}{12}bd^3 = 86.2\ \text{in}^4$; $I/c = \frac{1}{6}bd^2 = 23.1\ \text{in}^3$; and $A = bd = 18.60\ \text{in}^2$. Therefore

$$
\sigma_{\max} = \frac{M_{\max}}{I/c} = \frac{33,750}{23.1} = 1460\ \text{lb/in}^2 \qquad [\text{by Eq. } (8.1\text{-}12)]
$$

This stress occurs at $x = 75$ in and is tension in the bottom fibers of the beam and compression in the top. The maximum *transverse* shear stress is$^\dagger$

$$
\tau_{\max} = \frac{3}{2}\frac{V_{\max}}{A} = \frac{3}{2}\frac{1020}{18.60} = 82\ \text{lb/in}^2 \qquad [\text{by Eq. } (8.1\text{-}13)]
$$

which is the horizontal and vertical shear stress at the neutral axis of the section just to the left of the right support.

$(b)$ For the routed section it is found (Appendix A) that the neutral axis is 4 in from the base of the section and $I = 82.6\ \text{in}^4$.
