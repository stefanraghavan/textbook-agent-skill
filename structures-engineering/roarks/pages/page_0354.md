**14. Left end roller supported along an inclined surface at angle $\zeta$, right end fixed**

![Figure 14: Left end roller supported along an inclined surface at angle zeta, right end fixed](figures/fig14.png)
<!-- Description: A circular arch with the left end roller supported on an inclined surface at angle $\zeta$, showing force $P_A$ at the left end and displacement $\delta_I$ along the incline; right end fixed. -->

Since there is no deflection perpendicular to the incline and $M_A = 0$, the following equations give $P_A, \delta_I,$ and $\psi_A$:

$$
P_A
=
\frac{LF_V \cos \zeta - LF_H \sin \zeta}
{B_{HH}\sin^2 \zeta - 2B_{HV}\sin \zeta \cos \zeta + B_{VV}\cos^2 \zeta}
$$

$$
\delta_I
=
\frac{R^3}{EI}
\left\{
P_A
\left[
B_{HV}(\cos^2 \zeta - \sin^2 \zeta)
+
(B_{VV} - B_{HH})\sin \zeta \cos \zeta
\right]
-
LF_H \cos \zeta
-
LF_V \sin \zeta
\right\}
$$

$$
\psi_A
=
\frac{R^2}{EI}
\left[
P_A(B_{MV}\cos \zeta - B_{MH}\sin \zeta) - LF_M
\right]
$$

Use load terms given above for cases 5a–5s
