**6. Uniform temperature variation from top to bottom from $a$ to $l$**

![Figure 8.1: Uniform temperature variation from top to bottom from a to l](figures/fig8_1.png)
<!-- Description: Beam diagram showing coordinates, end reactions and moments $R_A$, $R_B$, $M_A$, $M_B$, end slopes $\theta_A$, $\theta_B$, end deflections $y_A$, temperature distribution with $T_1$ at top and $T_2$ at bottom from $a$ to $l$, and beam depth $t$. -->

Transverse shear $= V = R_A$

Bending moment $= M = M_A + R_A x$

Slope $= \theta = \theta_A + \dfrac{M_A x}{EI} + \dfrac{R_A x^2}{2EI} + \dfrac{\gamma}{t}(T_2 - T_1)(x-a)$

Deflection $= y = y_A + \theta_A x + \dfrac{M_A x^2}{2EI} + \dfrac{R_A x^3}{6EI} + \dfrac{\gamma}{2t}(T_2 - T_1)(x-a)^2$

$\gamma =$ temperature coefficient of expansion (unit strain/$^\circ$)  
$t =$ depth of beam
