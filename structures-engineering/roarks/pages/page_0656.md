**3c. Hydrostatic internal\*** pressure $q_1$ at the junction for $x_1 > 3/\lambda_1$.$\dagger$  
If $x_1 < 3/\lambda_1$ the discontinuity in pressure gradient introduces small deformations at the junction.

![Figure 13.4: hydrostatic internal pressure at the junction of a cylinder and sphere](figures/fig13_4.png)
<!-- Description: Sketch of a cylindrical shell joined to a spherical shell, showing hydrostatic pressure distribution $q_1$ and dimension $x_1$ measured along the cylinder to the junction. -->

*Note:* There is no axial load on the cylinder. An axial load on the right end of the sphere balances the axial component of pressure in the sphere and on the joint.

$$
LT_{A1}=\frac{b_1R_1}{t_1^2}
$$

$$
LT_{A2}=-\frac{b_2^2E_1\sin\phi_2}{E_2t_1t_2}
$$

For $LT_{AC}$ use the expressions from case 3a

$$
LT_{B1}=-\frac{b_1R_1}{x_1t_1}
$$

$$
LT_{B2}=\frac{E_1b_2R_2\sin\phi_2}{E_2t_2x_1}
$$

For $LT_{BC}$ use the expressions from case 3a

At the junction of the cylinder and sphere,

$$
V_1=q_1t_1K_{V1}, \qquad M_1=q_1t_1^2K_{M1}, \qquad N_1=0
$$

$$
\Delta R_A=\frac{q_1t_1}{E_1}\left(LT_{A1}-K_{V1}C_{AA1}-K_{M1}C_{AB1}\right)
$$

$$
\psi_A=\frac{q_1}{E_1}\left(-LT_{B1}+K_{V1}C_{AB1}+K_{M1}C_{BB1}\right)
$$

For internal pressure, $x_1=R_1$, $E_1=E_2$, $\nu_1=\nu_2=0.3$, $R_2\sin\phi_2=R_1$, and for $R/t>5$. *(Note: No correction terms are used)*

$$
\Delta R_A=\frac{q_1R_1^2}{E_1t_1}K_{\Delta RA}, \qquad
\psi_A=\frac{q_1R_1}{E_1t_1}K_{\psi A}, \qquad
\sigma_2=\frac{q_1R_1}{t_1}K_{\sigma 2}
$$
