# TABLE 13.4 Formulas for discontinuity stresses and deformations at the junctions of shells and plates *(Continued)*

**7c.** Hydrostatic internal\* pressure $q_1$ at the junction when $|\mu| > 4\dagger$ at position of zero pressure. If $|\mu| < 4$ at this position, the discontinuity in pressure gradient introduces small deformations at the junction.

![Figure 13.4: Hydrostatic internal pressure at cone-plate junction](figures/fig13_4.png)
<!-- Description: A cone joined to a plate at radius R2, showing hydrostatic pressure q1 varying along the cone over distance x1, with dimensions x1, w2, and R2 indicated. -->

$$
LT_{A1}=\frac{b_1R_1}{t_1^2\cos\alpha_1}
$$

$$
LT_{A2}=\frac{E_1t_2b_1^2}{32D_2t_1R_1}
$$

For $K_{P2}$ use the expressions from case 7a

For $LT_{AC}$ use the expression from case 7a

$$
LT_{B1}=\frac{-b_1}{t_1\cos\alpha_1}\left(\frac{R_1}{x_1}+2\tan\alpha_1\right)
$$

$$
LT_{B2}=\frac{E_1b_1^2}{16D_2R_1}K_{P2}
$$

For $LT_{BC}$ use the expression from case 7a

*Note:* There is no axial load on the junction. An axial load on the left end of the cone balances any axial component of the pressure on the cone, and the axial load on the plate is reacted by the annular line load

$$
u_2=q_1b_1^2/(2R_2)
$$

at a radius $R_2$.

At the junction of the cone and plate,

$$
V_1=q_1t_1K_{V1}, \qquad M_1=q_1t_1^2K_{M1}
$$

$$
N_1=-V_1\sin\alpha_1
$$

$$
\Delta R_A=\frac{q_1t_1}{E_1}(LT_{A1}-K_{V1}C_{AA1}-K_{M1}C_{AB1})
$$

$$
\psi_A=\frac{q_1}{E_1}(-LT_{B1}+K_{V1}C_{AB1}+K_{M1}C_{BB1})
$$

For internal pressure, $E_1=E_2$, $\nu_1=\nu_2=0.3$, $x_1=R_1$, $\alpha_2=\alpha_1$, $R_2=0.7R_1$, and for $R_1/t_1\cos\alpha > 5$ and $R_1/t_2 > 4$. (Note: No correction terms are used.)

$$
\Delta R_A=\frac{q_1R_1^2}{E_1t_1}K_{\Delta RA}, \qquad
\psi_A=\frac{q_1R_1}{E_1t_1}K_{\psi A}, \qquad
\sigma_2=\frac{q_1R_1}{t_1}K_{\sigma 2},
$$
