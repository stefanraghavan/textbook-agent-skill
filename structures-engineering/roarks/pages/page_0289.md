The desired vertical deflection of the loaded end can be determined from Castigliano's theorem, using Eq. (9.2-2) for $U_f$ in summation form rather than integral form. This reduces to

$$
\delta = \frac{\delta U}{\delta P} = \frac{P}{E} \sum \left[ \frac{(M/P)^2}{eR} + F\left(\frac{V}{P}\right)^2 2(1 + \nu) + \left(\frac{N}{P}\right)^2 - 2\frac{M}{P}\frac{N}{P} \right] \frac{\Delta l}{A}
$$

$$
= \frac{P}{E} \sum \frac{\Delta l}{A}[B]
$$

where $[B]$ and $[B]\Delta l/A$ are the last two columns in the following table. The internal forces and moments can be determined from equilibrium equations as

$$
\frac{M}{P} = -(1 - x_i), \qquad \theta_i = \tan^{-1}\frac{dx}{dy}, \qquad V = P\sin\theta_i, \qquad \text{and} \qquad N = -P\cos\theta_i
$$
