| **8. Off-center circular hole in a member of rectangular cross section** | **8a. Elastic stress, axial tension** | $\sigma_{\max}=\sigma_A=K_t\sigma_{\mathrm{nom}}$ |

![Figure 8: Off-center circular hole in a member of rectangular cross section](figures/fig8.png)
<!-- Description: A rectangular member with an off-center circular hole. Dimensions shown include $D$, $c$, and point $A$ at the lower edge of the hole. A side view indicates thickness $t$. -->

![Figure 8a: Elastic stress, axial tension](figures/fig8a.png)
<!-- Description: A rectangular member with an off-center circular hole loaded in axial tension by forces $P$ at both ends. Dimensions shown include $D/2$. -->

$$
\text{where }\sigma_{\mathrm{nom}}=\frac{P}{Dt}\frac{\sqrt{1-(r/c)^2}}{1-(r/c)}\frac{1-(c/D)}{1-(c/D)[2-\sqrt{1-(r/c)^2}]}
$$

$$
K_t=3.00-3.13\left(\frac{r}{c}\right)+3.66\left(\frac{r}{c}\right)^2-1.53\left(\frac{r}{c}\right)^3
$$

(Refs. 1 and 32)

|---|---|---|
|  | **8b. Elastic stress, in-plane bending** | $\sigma_{\max}=\sigma_A=K_t\sigma_{\mathrm{nom}}$ |

![Figure 8b: Elastic stress, in-plane bending](figures/fig8b.png)
<!-- Description: A rectangular member with an off-center circular hole subjected to equal end moments $M$ causing in-plane bending. -->

$$
\text{where }\sigma_{\mathrm{nom}}=\frac{12M}{tD^3}\left(\frac{D}{2}-c+r\right)
$$

and $K_t=3.0 \qquad \text{if } r/c<0.05$

or

$$
K_t=C_1+C_2\left(\frac{2c}{D}\right)+C_3\left(\frac{2c}{D}\right)^2+C_4\left(\frac{2c}{D}\right)^3
$$

where for $0.05\leq r/c\leq 0.5$

$$
C_1=3.022-0.422r/c+3.556(r/c)^2
$$

$$
C_2=-0.569+2.664r/c-4.397(r/c)^2
$$

$$
C_3=3.138-18.367r/c+28.093(r/c)^2
$$

$$
C_4=-3.591+16.125r/c-27.252(r/c)^2
$$

(Ref. 31)

| **9. Elliptical hole in an infinite plate** | **9a. Elastic stress, in-plane normal stress** | **(a1) Uniaxial stress, $\sigma_2=0$** |

![Figure 9: Elliptical hole in an infinite plate](figures/fig9.png)
<!-- Description: An infinite plate containing a vertical elliptical hole with semi-major dimension $a$, semi-minor dimension $b$, and radius of curvature at point $A$ labeled $r_A$. Relationship shown: $r_A=b^2/a$. -->

![Figure 9a: Elastic stress, in-plane normal stress](figures/fig9a.png)
<!-- Description: An infinite plate with a vertical elliptical hole subjected to in-plane normal stresses $\sigma_1$ horizontally and $\sigma_2$ vertically. Points $A$ and $B$ are marked on the hole boundary. -->

$$
r_A=\frac{b^2}{a}
$$

$$
\sigma_A=\left(1+\frac{2a}{b}\right)\sigma_1
\qquad \text{or} \qquad
\sigma_A=\left(1+2\sqrt{\frac{a}{r_A}}\right)\sigma_1
$$

$$
\sigma_B=-\sigma_1
$$

**(a2) Biaxial stress, $\sigma_2=\sigma_1$**

$$
\sigma_A=2\frac{a}{b}\sigma_1
$$

$$
\sigma_B=2\frac{b}{a}\sigma_1
$$

**(a3) Biaxial stress, $\sigma_2=-\sigma_1$**

$$
\sigma_A=2\left(1+\frac{a}{b}\right)\sigma_1
$$

$$
\sigma_B=-2\left(1+\frac{b}{a}\right)\sigma_1
$$

This stress condition would also be created by pure shear inclined at $45^\circ$ to the axes of the ellipse.
