[6.90]

With Eq. (6.90), a quick analytical evaluation of the ground roll can be made.

An analytic form for $s_g$ that more clearly illustrates the design parameters that govern takeoff performance can be obtained by substituting Eq. (6.82) directly into Eq. (6.81), obtaining

$$
ds = \frac{m}{2}\frac{d(V_\infty^2)}{T - D - \mu_r(W - L)}
$$

[6.91]

Integrating Eq. (6.91) from point 0 to liftoff, and again noting that $m = W/g$, we have

$$
s_g = \frac{W}{2g}\int_0^{V_{LO}} \frac{d(V_\infty^2)}{T - D - \mu_r(W - L)}
$$

[6.92]

In Eq. (6.92), $T - D - \mu_r(W - L)$ is the net force acting in the horizontal direction on the airplane during takeoff.
