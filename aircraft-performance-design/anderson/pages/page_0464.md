The *exposed* planform areas of the wing and tail are the areas seen in the configuration layout, Fig. 8.27, and do *not* include the effective additional areas that project into the fuselage. For example, our calculated planform area of the wing, $S = 176\ \mathrm{ft}^2$ obtained in Section 8.4.2, includes that part of the wing which is projected inside the fuselage. The wing area shown in Fig. 8.19 includes the region covered by the fuselage; the area shown in Fig. 8.19 (since it is only one-half of the wing) is $176/2 = 88\ \mathrm{ft}^2$. In contrast, the value of $S_{\text{exposed wing planform}}$ is less than $176\ \mathrm{ft}^2$. From Figs. 8.10, 8.22, and 8.27, we obtain

$$
S_{\text{exposed wing planform}} = 148\ \mathrm{ft}^2
$$

$$
S_{\text{exposed horiz tail planform}} = 35.3\ \mathrm{ft}^2
$$

$$
S_{\text{exposed vert tail planform}} = 14.4\ \mathrm{ft}^2
$$

The center fuselage section, section B, is represented by a circular cylinder of diameter 4.28 ft. The rearward section, section C, is approximated by a right circular cone with a base diameter of 4.28 ft and an altitude of 9 ft.
