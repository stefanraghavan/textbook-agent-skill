*d.* The flight velocities for maximum values of the above aerodynamic ratios are related in magnitude as follows:

$$
V_{(C_L^{3/2}/C_D)_{\max}} : V_{(C_L/C_D)_{\max}} : V_{(C_L^{1/2}/C_D)_{\max}} = 0.76 : 1 : 1.32
$$

7. The stall speed of a given airplane at a given altitude is dictated by $(C_L)_{\max}$ and the wing loading:

$$
V_{\text{stall}} = \sqrt{\frac{2}{\rho_\infty}\frac{W}{S}\frac{1}{(C_L)_{\max}}}
$$

[5.67]

The values of $(C_L)_{\max}$ can be increased by a variety of high-lift devices, such as trailing- and leading-edge flaps, slats, etc.

8. Rate of climb is given by

$$
R/C = \frac{TV_\infty - DV_\infty}{W} = \frac{\text{excess power}}{W}
$$

[5.78]

The various analytical expressions obtained for a rate of climb analysis show that $R/C$ for a given airplane at a given altitude depends on wing loading and thrust-to-weight ratio.

9. For unpowered gliding flight, the glide angle $\theta$ is determined by

$$
\tan \theta = \frac{1}{L/D}
$$

[5.125]

10. Absolute ceiling is that altitude where $(R/C)_{\max} = 0$. Service ceiling is that altitude where $(R/C)_{\max} = 100$ ft/min.

11. The conditions for maximum range and maximum endurance are different. Moreover, they also depend on whether the airplane is propeller-driven or jet-propelled:

$$
R = \frac{\eta_{pr}}{c}\frac{L}{D}\ln\frac{W_0}{W_1}
$$

propeller-driven

[5.153]

$$
R = \frac{2}{c_t}\sqrt{\frac{2}{\rho_\infty S}}\frac{C_L^{1/2}}{C_D}\left(W_0^{1/2} - W_1^{1/2}\right)
$$

jet

[5.156]

$$
E = \frac{\eta_{pr}}{c}\sqrt{2\rho_\infty S}\frac{C_L^{3/2}}{C_D}\left(W_1^{-1/2} - W_0^{-1/2}\right)
$$

propeller-driven

[5.169]

$$
E = \frac{1}{c_t}\frac{L}{D}\ln\frac{W_0}{W_1}
$$

jet

[5.167]

Note that maximum endurance for a propeller-driven airplane occurs when the airplane is flying at $(C_L^{3/2}/C_D)_{\max}$. Maximum range for a propeller-driven airplane and maximum endurance for a jet occur when the airplane is flying at $(L/D)_{\max}$. Maximum range for a jet occurs when the airplane is flying at $(C_L^{1/2}/C_D)_{\max}$.
