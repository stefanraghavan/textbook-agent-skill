There is a strong altitude effect on thrust, as can be seen by examining Eq. (3.16). Again, we note that $\dot{m}_{air} = \rho_\infty A_1 V_\infty$; hence $\dot{m}_{air}$ is directly proportional to $\rho_\infty$. As the altitude increases, $\rho_\infty$ decreases. In turn, from Eq. (3.16) where $T$ is almost directly proportional to $\dot{m}_{air}$, thrust also decreases with altitude. Indeed, it is reasonable to express the variation of $T$ with altitude in terms of the density ratio $\rho/\rho_0$, where $\rho$ is the density at a given altitude and $\rho_0$ is sea-level density. Hence,

$$
\frac{T}{T_0} = \frac{\rho}{\rho_0}
$$

[3.19]

where $T_0$ is the sea-level thrust.
