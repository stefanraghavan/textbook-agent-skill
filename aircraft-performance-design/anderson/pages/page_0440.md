**Figure 8.10** Scale plan view of our wing design.

The planform shape is a trapezoid. The area of a trapezoid is given by $\frac{1}{2}(a + b)h$, where $a$ and $b$ are the two parallel sides and $h$ is the altitude. The area shown in Fig. 8.10 is one-half the total wing area, and the parallel sides $a$ and $b$ are $c_t$ and $c_r$. The altitude is $b/2$. Hence, from the formula for the trapezoidal area, we have

$$
\frac{S}{2} = \frac{1}{2}(c_t + c_r)\frac{b}{2}
$$

or

$$
2S = (c_t + c_r)b
$$

[8.55]

Dividing Eq. (8.55) by $c_r$, we have

$$
\frac{2S}{c_r} = (\lambda + 1)b
$$

or

$$
c_r = \frac{2S}{(\lambda + 1)b} = \frac{2(176)}{(0.5 + 1)(35.27)} = 6.65\ \text{ft}
$$

and

$$
c_t = \lambda c_r = 0.5(6.65) = 3.325\ \text{ft}
$$

The wing semispan is

$$
\frac{b}{2} = \frac{35.27}{2} = 17.64\ \text{ft}
$$

These are the dimensions shown in Fig. 8.10.
