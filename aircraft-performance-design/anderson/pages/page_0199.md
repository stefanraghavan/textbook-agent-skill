Figure 3.29 Block diagram summary.

Propulsion systems

Reciprocating engine/propeller combination

Power  
$P_A = h_{pr} P$  
$h_{pr} = f(J)$  
$J = \dfrac{V_\infty}{ND}$

Velocity variation  
Shaft power $P$ constant with $V_\infty$

Altitude variation  
1. $P/P_0 = r/r_0$  
2. (slightly more accurate) $P/P_0 = 1.132\, r/r_0 - 0.132$

Specific fuel consumption

Velocity variation  
SFC is constant with $V_\infty$

Altitude variation  
SFC is constant with altitude

Turbojet engine

Power  
$P_A = TV_\infty$

Velocity variation  
1. Subsonic: $T$ is constant with $V_\infty$  
2. Supersonic: $\dfrac{T}{T_{M=1}} = 1 + 1.18(M_\infty - 1)$

Altitude variation  
$T/T_0 = r/r_0$

Specific fuel consumption

Velocity variation  
1. Subsonic: TSFC = $1.0 + kM_\infty$  
2. Supersonic: TSFC is constant

Altitude variation  
TSFC is constant with altitude

Turbofan engine

Power  
$P_A = TV_\infty$

Velocity variation  
1. High bypass ratio: $T/T_{V_\infty=0} = AM_\infty^{-n}$  
2. Low bypass ratio: $T$ first increases with $M_\infty$, then decreases at higher supersonic $M_\infty$

Altitude variation  
$T/T_0 = (r/r_0)^m$

Specific fuel consumption

Velocity variation  
1. High bypass ratio: $c_t = B(1 + kM_\infty)$  
2. Low bypass ratio: $c_t$ gradually increases with velocity

Altitude variation  
$c_t$ is constant with altitude

Turboprop engine

Power  
$P_A = (T_p + T_j)V_\infty$  
$P_A = h_{pr} P_s + T_j \cdot V_\infty$  
$P_A = h_{pr} P_{es}$

Velocity variation  
$P_A$ is constant with $M_\infty$

Altitude variation  
$P_A/P_{A,0} = (r/r_0)^m$

Specific fuel consumption

Velocity variation  
$c_A$ is constant with $V_\infty$

Altitude variation  
$c_A$ is constant with altitude
