**Figure 5.13** Sketch of the variation of thrust available versus velocity for a propellor-driven aircraft.

Since power is given by force times velocity (see Section 3.2), from Eq. (3.3) the power available from any flight propulsion device is

$$
P_A = T_A V_\infty
$$

[5.47]

Combining Eqs. (3.13) and (5.47) and solving for $T_A$, we get

$$
T_A = \frac{\eta_{pr} P}{V_\infty}
$$

[5.48]

Similarly, for a turboprop, the power available is given by Eq. (3.29), repeated here:

$$
P_A = \eta_{pr} P_{es}
$$

[3.29]

Combining Eqs. (3.29) and (5.47) and solving for $T_A$, we have

$$
T_A = \frac{\eta_{pr} P_{es}}{V_\infty}
$$

[5.49]

Hence, for the given power ratings, the shaft power $P$ for a piston engine and the equivalent shaft power $P_{es}$ for a turboprop, Eqs. (5.48) and (5.49) give the thrust available for each type of power plant, respectively.
