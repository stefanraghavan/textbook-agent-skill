Substituting Eq. (5.130) into Eq. (5.128), we have

Dividing Eq. (5.124) by Eq. (5.123), we obtain

$$
\sin\theta=\frac{D}{L}\cos\theta=\frac{C_D}{C_L}\cos\theta
\eqno{[5.132]}
$$

Inserting Eq. (5.132) into Eq. (5.131), we have

$$
V_v=\sqrt{\frac{2\cos^3\theta}{\rho_\infty(C_L^3/C_D^2)}\frac{W}{S}}
\eqno{[5.133]}
$$

By making the assumption that $\cos\theta=1$, Eq. (5.133) is written as

$$
V_v=\sqrt{\frac{2}{\rho_\infty(C_L^3/C_D^2)}\frac{W}{S}}
\eqno{[5.134]}
$$

Equation (5.134) explicitly shows that $(V_v)_{\min}$ occurs at $(C_L^{3/2}/C_D)_{\max}$. It also shows that the sink rate decreases with decreasing altitude and increases as the square root of the wing loading.
