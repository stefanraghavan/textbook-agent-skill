Equation (6.71) is the equation of motion for the airplane during takeoff.

Let us examine the terms on the right-hand side of Eq. (6.71). The engine thrust $T$ in general varies with velocity during the ground roll. For a reciprocating engine/propeller combination, the power available is reasonably constant with $V_\infty$ (see Section 3.3.1). Since $P=TV_\infty$, during the ground roll,

Reciprocating engine/propeller  $T=\dfrac{\text{const}}{V_\infty}$  (6.72)

For a turbojet engine, $T$ is reasonably constant with $V_\infty$ for the ground roll (see Section 3.4.1).

Turbojet  $T=\text{const}$  (6.73)

For a turbofan engine, $T$ decreases slightly with $V_\infty$ during the ground roll. An example for the Rolls-Royce RB211-535E4 turbofan was given by Eq. (3.22). Following this example, we can write for a turbofan engine during the ground roll

Turbofan  $T=k_1^*-k_2^*V_\infty+k_3^*V_\infty^2$  (6.74)

where the values of $k_1^*$, $k_2^*$, and $k_3^*$ are constants obtained from the performance characteristics for a given engine.

$$
D=\frac{1}{2}\rho_\infty V_\infty^2SC_D
\tag{6.75}
$$
