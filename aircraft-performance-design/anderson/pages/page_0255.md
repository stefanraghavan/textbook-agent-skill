## 5.7.3 Maximum Velocity

Consider a propeller-driven airplane. The power available $P_A$ is essentially constant with velocity, as sketched in Fig. 5.18. The intersection of the maximum power available curve and the power required curve defines the maximum velocity for straight and level flight, as shown in Fig. 5.18.

Consider a jet-propelled airplane. Assuming $T_A$ is constant with velocity, the power available at subsonic speeds varies linearly with $V_\infty$ and is sketched in Fig. 5.19. The power required $P_R$ is also sketched in Fig. 5.19. The high-speed intersection of the maximum power available curve and the power required curve defines the maximum velocity for straight and level flight, as shown in Fig. 5.19.
