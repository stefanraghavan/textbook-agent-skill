# CHAPTER 8 • Design of a Propeller-Driven Airplane

The spanwise location of the mean aerodynamic chord for the horizontal tail is [see Eq. (8.57)]

$$
\bar{y}_{\mathrm{HT}}=\frac{b_t}{6}\frac{1+2\lambda}{1+\lambda}=\frac{12.2}{6}\frac{1+1}{1+0.5}=2.71\ \mathrm{ft}
$$

and the mean aerodynamic chord for the horizontal tail is [see Eq. (8.58)]

$$
\bar{c}_{\mathrm{HT}}=\frac{2}{3}c_{rt}\frac{1+\lambda+\lambda^2}{1+\lambda}=\frac{2}{3}(4.07)\frac{1.75}{1.5}=3.16\mathrm{ft}
$$

This allows us to lay out the horizontal tail as shown in Fig. 8.22a.

We now lay out the vertical tail. Typical aspect ratios for vertical tails $AR_{\mathrm{VT}}$ range from 1.3 to 2.0, where the aspect ratio is based on the root-to-tip height $h_{\mathrm{VT}}$ (span from tip to tip does not have any meaning here).
