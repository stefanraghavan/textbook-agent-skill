$M_\infty = 1.2$ can be obtained from the following equation:

$$
C_{D,W,0} = E_{WD}\left[1 - 0.386(M_\infty - 1.2)^{0.57}\left(1 - \frac{\pi \Lambda^{0.77}}{100}\right)\right]\times [C_{D,W,0}]_{\text{Sears-Haack}}
$$

[5.63]

where $\Lambda$ is the sweep angle of the wing leading edge, $E_{WD}$ is an empirical wave drag efficiency factor, and $(C_{D,W,0})_{\text{Sears-Haack}}$ is the theoretical zero-lift wave drag coefficient for the minimum drag Sears-Haack body of revolution, as described in Ref. 46, where

$$
[C_{D,W,0}]_{\text{Sears-Haack}} = \frac{9\pi}{2S}\left[\frac{A_{\max}}{l}\right]^2
$$

[5.64]

In Eq. (5.64), $S$ is the wing area, $A_{\max}$ is the maximum cross-sectional area, and $l$ is the longitudinal dimension of the body, reduced by the length of any portion of the body with constant cross-sectional area.
