## Delta Wings

Swept wings that have planforms such as shown in Fig. 2.32 are called *delta wings*. Interest in delta wings for airplanes goes as far back as the early work done by Alexander Lippisch in Germany during the 1930s. Delta wings are employed on many aircraft designed for supersonic flight, for example, the F-102 (Fig. 1.31) and the Concorde (Fig. 1.35). The supersonic lifting characteristics of delta wings are essentially given by the data in Fig. 2.31, which have already been discussed in the previous section. In this section we concentrate on the subsonic flow over delta wings.
