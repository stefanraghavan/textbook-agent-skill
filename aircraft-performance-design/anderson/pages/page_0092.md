finite wing made up of the NACA 2412 airfoil section, also at an angle of attack of $4^\circ$. Is the value of $C_L$ for the wing also 0.65? The answer is no. The reason for the difference is that there are strong vortices produced at the wing tips of the finite wing, which trail downstream. These vortices are analogous to minitornadoes, and like a tornado, they reach out in the flow field and induce changes in the velocity and pressure fields around the wing. These wing-tip vortices are shown schematically in Fig. 2.21. Imagine that you are standing on top of the wing shown in Fig. 2.21.
