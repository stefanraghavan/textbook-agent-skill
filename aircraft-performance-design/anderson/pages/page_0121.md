Jobe (Ref. 27) recommends an alternate formula developed by White and Christoph in which $c_f$ is more easily calculated in an explicit manner from

$$c_f = \frac{0.42}{\ln^2(0.056\ \mathrm{Re})} \qquad [2.29]$$

Equation (2.29) is claimed to be accurate to $\pm 4\%$ in the Reynolds number range from $10^5$ to $10^9$. However, there remains the question as to *where* to apply the above formulas, which is a matter of where transition occurs. Equation (2.27) is valid as long as the flow is completely laminar. Equations (2.28) and (2.29) are applicable as long as the flow is completely turbulent.
