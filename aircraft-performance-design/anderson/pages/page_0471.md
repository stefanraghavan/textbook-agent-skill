# 8.8.6 Takeoff Distance

An estimate of the ground roll can be obtained from Eq. (6.95):

$$
s_g = \frac{1.21(W/S)}{g\rho_\infty (C_L)_{\max}(T/W)}
$$

[6.95]

In Eq. (6.95), an average value of $T/W$ during takeoff is that value at $V_\infty = 0.7V_{LO}$, where $V_{LO} = 1.1V_{\text{stall}}$. Hence, $T/W$ is evaluated at a velocity of

$$
V_\infty = 0.7V_{LO} = 0.77V_{\text{stall}} = 0.77(91.5) = 70.4\ \text{ft/s}
$$

Since the power available is, from Eq. (3.13),

$$
P_A = \eta_{pr}P = T_A V_\infty
$$

we have (recalling $HP = 360$, and $550\ \text{ft-lb/s}$ is 1 hp)

$$
T_A = \frac{\eta_{pr}P}{V_\infty} = \frac{(0.8)(360)(550)}{70.4} = 2,250\ \text{lb}
$$

Hence,

$$
\left(\frac{T}{W}\right)_{0.7V_{LO}} = \frac{2,250}{4,100} = 0.549
$$

Returning to Eq. (6.95), and recalling that $(C_L)_{\max} = 1.98$ with the flaps in the takeoff position, and $W/S = 23.3\ \text{lb/ft}^2$, we have

$$
s_g = \frac{1.21(W/S)}{g\rho_\infty (C_L)_{\max}(T/W)} = \frac{1.21(23.3)}{(32.2)(0.002377)(1.98)(0.549)} = 338.9\ \text{ft}
$$

To obtain the distance covered while airborne to clear an obstacle, we first calculate the flight path radius from Eq. (6.98).

$$
R = \frac{6.96(V_{\text{stall}})^2}{g} = \frac{(6.96)(91.5)^2}{32.2} = 1,810\ \text{ft}
$$

From Eq. (6.99), the included flight path angle is

$$
\theta_{OB} = \text{Cos}^{-1}\left(1 - \frac{h_{OB}}{R}\right)
$$

[6.99]

where $h_{OB}$ is the obstacle height, $h_{OB} = 50\ \text{ft}$.

$$
\theta_{OB} = \text{Cos}^{-1}\left(1 - \frac{50}{1,810}\right) = 13.5^\circ
$$

From Eq. (6.100), the airborne distance is

$$
s_a = R\sin\theta_{OB} = 1,810\sin13.5^\circ = 422.5\ \text{ft}
$$

The total takeoff distance is then

$$
\text{Takeoff distance} = s_g + s_a = 338.9 + 422.5 = \boxed{761.4\ \text{ft}}
$$

This is far less than the specified takeoff distance of 2,500 ft as stated in Section 8.2.
