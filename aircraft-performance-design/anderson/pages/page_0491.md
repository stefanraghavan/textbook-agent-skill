Indeed, even today the size of the vertical tail of multiengine airplanes, propeller- or jet-powered, is usually dictated by consideration of engine-out performance. Also, engine-out performance dictates in part the lateral location of the engines on the wing. The closer the engines to the fuselage, the smaller the moment about the vertical axis when an engine fails. Of course, for a propeller-driven airplane, the engines must be far enough away to allow sufficient propeller clearance with the fuselage.
