For section A, the surface area of the elliptical base is $\pi ab = \pi (4.28/2)(2.93/2)$ $= 9.85\ \mathrm{ft}^2$, where $a$ and $b$ are the semimajor and semiminor axes, respectively. The surface area of the side of the cylinder is the circumference times the length of the side. The circumference of the elliptical base is approximately $2\pi \sqrt{(a^2 + b^2)/2} = 2\pi \sqrt{[(2.14)^2 + (1.465)^2]/2} = 11.52\ \mathrm{ft}$. Hence, the surface area of the side of the elliptical cylinder is $(11.52)(6.75) = 77.8\ \mathrm{ft}^2$. The total wetted surface area of section A is therefore $9.85 + 77.8 = 87.63\ \mathrm{ft}^2$. For section B, the area of the base of the circular cylinder is $\pi d^2/4 = \pi (4.28)^2/4 = 14.39\ \mathrm{ft}^2$. The exposed wetted surface area of the base is that outside of the intersection with section A, namely, $14.39 - 9.85 = 4.54\ \mathrm{ft}^2$. The surface area of the side of the circular cylinder is $\pi (4.28)(11.3) = 151.9\ \mathrm{ft}^2$. Hence the total wetted surface area of section B is $4.54 + 151.9 = 156.5\ \mathrm{ft}^2$. The surface area of the cone designated as section C is given by $\pi r\sqrt{r^2 + h^2}$ where $r$ is the radius of the base and $h$ is the altitude. From Fig. 8.28, this surface area is $\pi (2.14)\sqrt{(2.14)^2 + (9)^2} = 62.2\ \mathrm{ft}^2$. Finally, the total wetted surface area of the geometric figure in Fig. 8.28 is

$$
S_{\text{wetted area}} = \text{section A} + \text{section B} + \text{section C}
$$

$$
= 87.63 + 156.5 + 62.2 = 306.3\ \mathrm{ft}^2
$$

Since Fig. 8.28 represents a crude estimate for the wetted surface area of the fuselage, we will use for this wetted area the value of $306.3\ \mathrm{ft}^2$ calculated above.
