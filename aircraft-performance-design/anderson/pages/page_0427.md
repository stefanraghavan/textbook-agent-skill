that is, corresponding for our case to $(C_L)_{\max} = 1.98$. From Eq. (5.67)

$$
V_{\text{stall}} = \sqrt{\frac{2}{\rho_\infty}\frac{W}{S}\frac{1}{(C_L)_{\max}}}
= \sqrt{\frac{2(29.3)}{(0.002377)(1.98)}} = 111.6 \text{ ft/s}
$$

From Eq. (6.98), the flight path radius is

$$
R = \frac{6.96(V_{\text{stall}})^2}{g}
= \frac{6.96(111.6)^2}{32.2} = 2,692 \text{ ft}
$$

From Eq. (6.99), the included flight path angle is

$$
\theta_{OB} = \cos^{-1}\left(1 - \frac{h_{OB}}{R}\right)
\qquad \text{[6.99]}
$$

where $h_{OB}$ is the obstacle height, $h_{OB} = 50$ ft, so

$$
\theta_{OB} = \cos^{-1}\left(1 - \frac{50}{2,692}\right) = 11.06^\circ
$$

From Eq. (6.100), the airborne distance is

$$
s_a = R \sin \theta_{OB} = 2,692 \sin 11.06^\circ = 516.4 \text{ ft}
\qquad \text{[8.35]}
$$

Combining Eqs. (8.34) and (8.35), we have

$$
s_g + s_a = 2,500 = \frac{233.9}{T/W} + 516.4
$$

or

$$
\left(\frac{T}{W}\right)_{0.7V_{LO}} = \frac{233.9}{2,500 - 516.4} = 0.118
\qquad \text{[8.36]}
$$

This is the value of required $T/W$ at a velocity

$$
V_\infty = 0.7V_{LO} = 0.7(1.1V_{\text{stall}}) = 0.7(1.1)(111.6) = 85.9 \text{ ft/s}
$$

At this velocity, the power required to take off at the gross weight $W_0 = 5,158$ lb [see Eq. (8.23)] is

$$
P_R = TV_\infty = \frac{T}{W}W_0V_\infty = (0.118)(5,158)(85.9) = 5.228 \times 10^4 \text{ ft}\!\cdot\!\text{lb/s}
\qquad \text{[8.37]}
$$

This power required must equal the power available $P_A$, obtained from Eq. (3.13).

$$
P_A = \eta_{pr}P
\qquad \text{[3.13]}
$$

Solving Eq. (3.13) for the shaft brake power $P$, we have

$$
P = \frac{P_A}{\eta_{pr}}
\qquad \text{[8.38]}
$$
