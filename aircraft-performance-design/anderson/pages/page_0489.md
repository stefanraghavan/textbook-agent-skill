Having made his point about the relative safety of a twin-engine airplane capable of flying on one engine, Douglas turned to the flight performance of such an aircraft. Of particular note was the stability and control of a twin-engine airplane with one engine out. Because of its relevance to airplane design, and because we have not studied the effects of engine-out performance on airplane design to this stage in this book, we pursue further some of Douglas's thoughts on this matter.
