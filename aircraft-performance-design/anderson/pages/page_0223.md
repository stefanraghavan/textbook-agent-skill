The drag (hence $T_R$) for a given airplane in steady, level flight is a function of altitude (denoted by $h$), velocity, and weight:

$$
D = f(h, V_{\infty}, W)
\qquad [5.9]
$$

This makes sense. When the altitude $h$ changes, so does density $\rho_{\infty}$; hence $D$ changes. Clearly, as $V_{\infty}$ changes, $D$ changes. As $W$ changes, so does the lift $L$; in turn, the induced drag (drag due to lift) changes, and hence the total drag changes.
