The dominant features of the 707—the 35° swept wing and the jet engines mounted in pods slung under the wing on struts—had been pioneered with the B-47. The major design changes reflected in the 707 were the low-wing configuration to allow a long body deck for carrying passengers or freight, and the use of a tricycle landing gear. The reason for the tricycle gear was purely and simply because airplane pilots were familiar with this type of landing gear; it allowed them to lift the nose at takeoff and depress the nose at landing.
