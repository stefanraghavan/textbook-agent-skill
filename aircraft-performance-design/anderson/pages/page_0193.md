However, the net effect of the combined variation of thrust and \(V_\infty\) in Fig. 3.25 is to yield, to a first approximation, a relatively flat variation of \(P_A\) with \(M_\infty\). $$
P_A \text{ is constant with } M_\infty

$$
\frac{P_A}{P_{A,0}}=\left(\frac{\rho}{\rho_0}\right)^n \qquad n=0.7

For other turboprop engines, the value of \(n\) in Eq. (3.36) will be slightly different.

Typical variations of the specific fuel consumption as a function of \(M_\infty\) and altitude are shown as the upper set of curves in Fig. 3.26, obtained from the data of Ref. 40. The specific fuel consumption shown here is \(c_A\) defined by Eq. (3.33). For all practical purposes, the results in Fig. 3.26 show that

$$
c_A \text{ is constant with both velocity and altitude}
$$

The lower set of curves shown in Fig. 3.26 gives the ratio of jet power to total power

$$
\frac{T_jV_\infty}{P_A}=\frac{T_jV_\infty}{(T_p+T_j)V_\infty}=\frac{T_j}{T_p+T_j}
$$
