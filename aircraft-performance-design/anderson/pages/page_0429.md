Let us estimate the value of $K$ to be consistent with the earlier assumed value of $(L/D)_{\max} = 14$ [see Eq. (8.11)]. From Eq. (5.30),

$$
\left(\frac{L}{D}\right)_{\max} = \sqrt{\frac{1}{4C_{D,0}K}}
\qquad \text{[5.30]}
$$

we have

$$
K = \frac{1}{4C_{D,0}(L/D)_{\max}^{2}} = \frac{1}{4(0.017)(14)^{2}}
$$

or

$$
\fbox{$K = 0.075$}
\qquad \text{[8.42]}
$$

This estimate for $K$ also allows an estimate of the aspect ratio, as follows. It is conventional to define another efficiency factor, the Oswald efficiency $e_o$, as

$$
\frac{C_L^2}{\pi e_o AR} \equiv k_1 + k_2 + \frac{C_L^2}{\pi e AR} = KC_L^2
\qquad \text{[8.43]}
$$

A reasonable estimate for $e_o$ for a low-wing general aviation airplane is 0.6 (see McCormick, Ref. 50). From Eq. (8.42),

$$
AR = \frac{1}{\pi e_o K} = \frac{1}{\pi(0.6)(0.075)}
$$

or

$$
\fbox{$AR = 7.07$}
\qquad \text{[8.44]}
$$

Finally, to return to the consideration of rate of climb, Eq. (5.122) gives an expression for maximum rate of climb for a propeller-driven airplane as

$$
(R/C)_{\max} = \frac{\eta_{pr}P}{W} - \left(\frac{2}{\rho_{\infty}}\sqrt{\frac{K}{3C_{D,0}}}\frac{W}{S}\right)^{1/2} \frac{1.155}{(L/D)_{\max}}
\qquad \text{[5.122]}
$$

Solving for the power term, we have

$$
\frac{\eta_{pr}P}{W} = (R/C)_{\max} + \left(\frac{2}{\rho_{\infty}}\sqrt{\frac{K}{3C_{D,0}}}\frac{W}{S}\right)^{1/2} \frac{1.155}{(L/D)_{\max}}
\qquad \text{[8.45]}
$$

Everything on the right side of Eq. (8.45) is known, including $(R/C)_{\max}$ which from the specifications is 1,000 ft/min = 16.67 ft/s at sea level. Hence, from Eq. (8.45),

$$
\frac{\eta_{pr}P}{W} = 16.67 + \left[\frac{2}{0.002377}\sqrt{\frac{0.075}{3(0.017)}}(29.3)\right]^{1/2} \frac{1.155}{14}
\qquad \text{[8.45a]}
$$

$$
\frac{\eta_{pr}P}{W} = 16.67 + 14.26 = 30.93 \text{ ft/s}
$$
