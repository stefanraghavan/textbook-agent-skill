It is worthwhile studying these drag polars, just to obtain a feeling for the numbers for $C_L$ and $C_D$. Also, note that each of the drag polars in Figs. 2.60 and 2.61 is for a given Mach number (or Mach number range). It is important to remember that $C_L$ and $C_D$ are functions of the Mach number; hence the same airplane will have different drag polars for different Mach numbers. At low subsonic Mach numbers, the differences will be small and can be ignored.
