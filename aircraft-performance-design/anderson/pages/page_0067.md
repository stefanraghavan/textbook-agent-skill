for parts (a) and (b) in Fig. 2.4, $M_{LE}$ is different from $M_{c/4}$ simply because we have chosen a different point about which to take the moments. *Important:* By convention, a moment which tends to rotate the body so as to increase the angle of attack is considered *positive*. The moments shown in Fig. 2.4 are drawn in the positive sense; that is, they tend to pitch the nose upward. Depending on the shape of the body, the moments can be either positive or negative. (In reality, for the positively cambered airfoil shown in Fig. 2.4, the moments will be pitch-down moments; that is, $M_{c/4}$ and $M_{LE}$ will be negative values and will act in the opposite direction from that shown in Fig. 2.4.)
