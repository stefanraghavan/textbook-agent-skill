# CHAPTER 5 • Airplane Performance: Steady Flight

*a.* Maximum $L/D$ occurs when the zero-lift drag equals the drag due to lift:

$$
C_{D,0} = K C_L^2
$$

[5.28]

The value of $(L/D)_{\max}$ depends only on $C_{D,0}$ and $K$.

$$
\left(\frac{L}{D}\right)_{\max} = \left(\frac{C_L}{C_D}\right)_{\max} = \sqrt{\frac{1}{4C_{D,0}K}}
$$

[5.30]

The flight velocity at which $(L/D)_{\max}$ is achieved for a given airplane depends on the altitude and wing loading:

$$
V_{(L/D)_{\max}} = \left(\frac{2}{\rho_\infty}\sqrt{\frac{K}{C_{D,0}}}\frac{W}{S}\right)^{1/2}
$$

[5.34]

Minimum $T_R$ occurs when $L/D$ is maximum.

*b.* Maximum $C_L^{3/2}/C_D$ occurs when the zero-lift drag is one-third of the drag due to lift:

$$
C_{D,0} = \frac{1}{3}KC_L^2
$$

[5.36]

The value of $(C_L^{3/2}/C_D)_{\max}$ depends only on $C_{D,0}$ and $K$:

$$
\left(\frac{C_L^{3/2}}{C_D}\right)_{\max} = \frac{1}{4}\left(\frac{3}{KC_{D,0}^{1/3}}\right)^{3/4}
$$

[5.38]

The flight velocity at which $(C_L^{3/2}/C_D)_{\max}$ is achieved for a given airplane depends on the altitude and wing loading:

$$
V_{(C_L^{3/2}/C_D)_{\max}} = \left(\frac{2}{\rho_\infty}\sqrt{\frac{K}{3C_{D,0}}}\frac{W}{S}\right)^{1/2}
$$

[5.41]

Minimum $P_R$ occurs when $C_L^{3/2}/C_D$ is maximum.

*c.* Maximum $C_L^{1/2}/C_D$ occurs when the zero-lift drag is 3 times the drag due to lift:

$$
C_{D,0} = 3KC_L^2
$$

[5.43]

The value of $(C_L^{1/2}/C_D)_{\max}$ depends only on $C_{D,0}$ and $K$:

$$
\left(\frac{C_L^{1/2}}{C_D}\right)_{\max} = \frac{3}{4}\left(\frac{1}{3KC_{D,0}^3}\right)^{1/4}
$$

[5.44]

The flight velocity at which $(C_L^{1/2}/C_D)_{\max}$ is achieved for a given airplane depends on the altitude and wing loading:

$$
V_{(C_L^{1/2}/C_D)_{\max}} = \left(\frac{2}{\rho_\infty}\sqrt{\frac{3K}{C_{D,0}}}\frac{W}{S}\right)^{1/2}
$$
