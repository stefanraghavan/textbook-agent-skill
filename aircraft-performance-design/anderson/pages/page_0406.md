### Curve A: Takeoff Constraint

If the requirements specify a maximum takeoff length, then curve $A$ gives the allowed variation of $T_0/W_0$ versus $W_0/S$ for which this requirement is exactly satisfied. For example, for simplicity, let us approximate the takeoff distance by the expression for the ground roll given by Eq. (6.95), repeated here:

$$
s_g = \frac{1.21(W/S)}{g\rho_\infty(C_L)_{\max}(T/W)}
$$

[6.95]

In Eq. (6.95), $s_g$ is a given number. Solving Eq. (6.95) for $T/W$, we have

$$
\frac{T}{W} = \left[\frac{1.21}{g\rho_\infty(C_L)_{\max}s_g}\right]\frac{W}{S}
$$
