# CHAPTER 5 • Airplane Performance: Steady Flight

From Eq. (5.36), *when* $C_L^{3/2}/C_D$ *is a maximum value, the zero-lift drag equals one-third the drag due to lift.* Furthermore, the value of $(C_L^{3/2}/C_D)_{\max}$ can be found by writing Eq. (5.36) as

$$
C_L=\sqrt{3C_{D,0}/K}
$$

$$
\tag{5.37}
$$

and substituting Eqs. (5.36) and (5.37) into Eq. (5.35). [Keep in mind that since Eqs. (5.36) and (5.37) hold only for the condition of maximum $C_L^{3/2}/C_D$, then Eq. (5.35) with these substitutions yields the value of maximum $C_L^{3/2}/C_D$.]

$$
\left(\frac{C_L^{3/2}}{C_D}\right)_{\max}
=
\left(\frac{C_L^{3/2}}{C_{D,0}+KC_L^2}\right)_{\max}
=
\frac{(3C_{D,0}/K)^{3/4}}{C_{D,0}+3C_{D,0}}
=
\frac{1}{4C_{D,0}}\left(\frac{3C_{D,0}}{K}\right)^{3/4}
$$

or

$$
\left(\frac{C_L^{3/2}}{C_D}\right)_{\max}
=
\frac{1}{4}\left(\frac{3}{K\,C_{D,0}^{1/3}}\right)^{3/4}
$$

$$
\tag{5.38}
$$

Note that the maximum value of $C_L^{3/2}/C_D$ is a function only of the drag polar, that is, $C_{D,0}$ and $K$.

In straight and level flight, where $L=W$, the velocity at which $(C_L^{3/2}/C_D)_{\max}$ is achieved can be found as follows.

$$
L=W=\frac{1}{2}\rho_\infty V_\infty^2 S C_L
$$

$$
\tag{5.39}
$$

When $C_L^{3/2}/C_D$ is a maximum, Eq. (5.37) holds. Substituting Eq. (5.37) into (5.39), and denoting the velocity at which $C_L^{3/2}/C_D$ is a maximum by $V_{(C_L^{3/2}/C_D)_{\max}}$, we have

$$
W=\frac{1}{2}\rho_\infty V_{(C_L^{3/2}/C_D)_{\max}}^2 S \sqrt{\frac{3C_{D,0}}{K}}
$$

$$
\tag{5.40}
$$

Solving Eq. (5.40) for the velocity, we obtain

$$
V_{(C_L^{3/2}/C_D)_{\max}}
=
\left(\frac{2}{\rho_\infty}\sqrt{\frac{K}{3C_{D,0}}}\frac{W}{S}\right)^{1/2}
$$

$$
\tag{5.41}
$$

Comparing Eq. (5.41) with (5.22) for $V_{(L/D)_{\max}}$, we see that

$$
V_{(C_L^{3/2}/C_D)_{\max}}
=
\left(\frac{1}{3}\right)^{1/4}V_{(L/D)_{\max}}
$$

or

$$
V_{(C_L^{3/2}/C_D)_{\max}}=0.76V_{(L/D)_{\max}}
$$

$$
\tag{5.42}
$$
