Eclipsed by jet-propelled airplanes, the design of heavy reciprocating engine/propeller-driven airplanes in the gross weight class above 10,000 lb has virtually ceased since the 1950s. The last major airplanes of this class were the Douglas DC-7 and the Lockheed Super Constellation, both large, relatively luxurious passenger transports.
