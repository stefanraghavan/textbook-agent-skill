Multiplying Equation 16.33 by $\dot{w}_1^2/\dot{w}_2^2$ produces the loss coefficient for converging flow into branch in terms of the velocity at point 2:

$$
K_{212}=\left(0.81-1.16\sqrt{\frac{r}{d}}+0.50\frac{r}{d}\right)\frac{\dot{w}_2^2}{\dot{w}_1^2}-\left(0.95-1.65\frac{r}{d}\right)\frac{\dot{w}_2}{\dot{w}_1}+1.34-1.69\frac{r}{d}.
$$

(16.34)

The energy equation in terms of the velocity at point 2 may be written as:

$$
P_2-P_1=\frac{\dot{w}_2^2}{2g\rho_w A_2^2}\left(K_{212}-1+\frac{\dot{w}_1^2 d_2^4}{\dot{w}_2^2 d_1^4}\right).
$$

By substituting Equation 16.34 into the energy equation, the pressure drop equation for converging flow into branch in terms of the velocity at point 2 is:

$$
P_2-P_1=\frac{\dot{w}_2^2}{2g\rho_w A_2^2}\left[\left(1.81-1.16\sqrt{\frac{r}{d}}+0.50\frac{r}{d}\right)\frac{\dot{w}_1^2}{\dot{w}_2^2}-\left(0.95-1.65\frac{r}{d}\right)\frac{\dot{w}_1}{\dot{w}_2}+0.34-1.69\frac{r}{d}\right].
$$

(16.35)

Loss coefficient $K_{212}$ for converging flow into the branch employing Equation 16.34 can be determined from Diagram 16.15. For converging flow from the other side run, replace subscript 2 by subscript 3 in Equations 16.34 and 16.35.
