## F.2 STREET, WATTERS, AND VENNARD VELOCITY PROFILE DERIVATION

Street et al. [3] give the following relations for rough pipes (their eqs. 9.29, 9.30, and 9.31, respectively):

$$
\frac{u}{V_*} = 5.75 \log_{10}\frac{y}{\epsilon} + 8.5,
$$
(F.1)

$$
\frac{V}{V_*} = 5.75 \log_{10}\frac{R}{\epsilon} + 4.75,
$$
(F.2)

$$
\frac{1}{\sqrt{f}} = 2.0 \log_{10}\frac{d}{\epsilon} + 1.14.
$$
(F.3)

By solving Equation F.3 for $1/\epsilon$ we obtain:

$$
\frac{1}{\epsilon} = \frac{10^{\frac{1}{2\sqrt{f}}-0.57}}{d} = \frac{10^{\frac{1}{2\sqrt{f}}-0.57}}{2R} = \frac{10^{1/(2\sqrt{f})-0.57}}{2R}.
$$
(F.4)

For simplicity let us represent $10^{1/(2\sqrt{f})-0.57}$ by the symbol $\xi$ temporarily; then, we have:

$$
\frac{1}{\epsilon} = \frac{\xi}{d} = \frac{\xi}{2R}.
$$
(F.5)

Substituting Equation F.5 into Equations F.1 and F.2, we have:

$$
\frac{u}{V_*} = 5.75 \log_{10}\frac{y}{\epsilon} + 8.5 = 5.75 \log_{10}\left[y\frac{\xi}{2R}\right] + 8.5,
$$
(F.1 Alt)

$$
\frac{V}{V_*} = 5.75 \log_{10}\frac{R}{\epsilon} + 4.75 = 5.75 \log_{10}\left[R\frac{\xi}{2R}\right] + 4.75
$$

$$
= 5.75 \log_{10}\left[\frac{\xi}{2}\right] + 4.75,
$$
(F.2 Alt)

then dividing Equation F.1 Alt by Equation F.2 Alt we obtain:

$$
\frac{u}{V} = \frac{u/V_*}{V/V_*} = \frac{5.75 \log_{10}\left[\dfrac{y}{R}\dfrac{\xi}{2}\right] + 8.5}{5.75 \log_{10}\left[\dfrac{\xi}{2}\right] + 4.75},
$$

$$
= \frac{\log_{10}\left[\dfrac{y}{R}\dfrac{\xi}{2}\right] + \dfrac{8.5}{5.75}}{\log_{10}\left[\dfrac{\xi}{2}\right] + \dfrac{4.75}{5.75}}
= \frac{\log_{10}\dfrac{y}{R} + \log_{10}\left[\dfrac{\xi}{2}\right] + 1.478261}{\log_{10}\left[\dfrac{\xi}{2}\right] + 0.826087}.
$$
(F.6)

But:

$$
\log_{10}\left[\frac{\xi}{2}\right] = \log_{10}[\xi] - \log_{10}2.
$$
(F.7)

Remembering that $10^{1/(2\sqrt{f})-0.57} = \xi$, we can rewrite Equation F.7 as:

$$
\log_{10}\left[\frac{10^{1/(2\sqrt{f})-0.57}}{2}\right] = \log_{10}\left(10^{1/(2\sqrt{f})-0.57}\right) - \log_{10}2.
$$
