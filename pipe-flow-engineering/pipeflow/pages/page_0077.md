## 6.2.2 Spherical Vessel

![FIGURE 6.2. Drain from a spherical vessel.](figures/page_0077_fig_01.png)

**FIGURE 6.2.** Drain from a spherical vessel.

The time to partially drain the vessel can be obtained by integrating Equation 6.4 within the limits $y = Y_I$ and $y = Y_{Int}$, where $Y_{Int}$ is an intermediate height between $Y_I$ and 0. Thus, the equation for a partially drained upright cylindrical vessel is:

$$
t = \frac{2\pi R^2}{\sqrt{\dfrac{2gA_P^2}{K_P}}}\left(\sqrt{C_{\Delta H}+Y_I}-\sqrt{C_{\Delta H}+Y_{Int}}\right).
$$

A degree of complexity is added to the drain problem when considering a spherical vessel because the cross-sectional area of the vessel varies with height. A spherical vessel of radius $R$ is shown in Figure 6.2. The initial liquid surface height $Y_I$ may be located anywhere between $+R$ and $-R$. Gravity and pressure draining occur from a pipe outlet located a vertical distance $Y_D$ from the bottom of the vessel. The vessel may be pressurized at a constant value $P_V$ and the pressure at the pipe discharge may be $P_D$. Determine the time $t$ (in seconds) required to drain the vessel.

As was the case for the cylindrical vessel, the change of liquid mass within the vessel can be expressed as:

$$
\dot{w}=\rho_w A_v \frac{dy}{dt},
$$

(6.1, repeated)

where $\rho_w$ is the density of the fluid. The cross-sectional area of the vessel $A_v$ varies with height $y$ as expressed below:

$$
A_v=\pi\left(R^2-y^2\right).
$$

(6.6)

The flow rate $\dot{w}$ through the drain line can be expressed as:

$$
\dot{w}=\sqrt{\frac{2g\rho_w A_P^2}{K_P}\left[P_V-P_D+\rho_w(Y_D+R+y)\right]},
$$

(6.7)

where $A_P$ is the flow area of the pipe and $K_P$ is the loss coefficient of the drain line. Substitution and rearranging of Equations 6.1, 6.6 and 6.7 yields:

$$
dt=\frac{\pi\left(R^2-y^2\right)}{\sqrt{\dfrac{2gA_P^2}{K_P}}\sqrt{\dfrac{P_V-P_D}{\rho_w}+Y_D+R+y}}\,dy.
$$

(6.8)

The integral form of Equation 6.8 within the limits $y=Y_I$ and $y=-R$ is:

$$
\int dt=\frac{\pi}{\sqrt{\dfrac{2gA_P^2}{K_P}}}\int_{-R}^{Y_I}\frac{R^2-y^2}{\sqrt{C_{\Delta H}+R+y}}\,dy,
$$

(6.9)

where

$$
C_{\Delta H}=\frac{P_V-P_D}{\rho_w}+Y_D.
$$

Integration of Equation 6.9 yields the drain time for a spherical vessel:

$$
t=\frac{2\pi R^2}{\sqrt{\dfrac{2gA_P^2}{K_P}}}\left(\sqrt{C_{\Delta H}+R+Y_I}-\sqrt{C_{\Delta H}}\right)
$$

$$
-\frac{2\pi}{15\sqrt{\dfrac{2gA_P^2}{K_P}}}
\left\{
\frac{\left[8(C_{\Delta H}+R)^2-4(C_{\Delta H}+R)Y_I+3Y_I^2\right]}
{\sqrt{C_{\Delta H}+R+Y_I}}
-
\frac{\left[8(C_{\Delta H}+R)^2+4(C_{\Delta H}+R)R+3R^2\right]}
{\sqrt{C_{\Delta H}}}
\right\}.
$$

The time to *partially* drain the vessel can be obtained by integrating Equation 6.9 within the limits $y=Y_I$ and $y=Y_{Int}$, where $Y_{Int}$ is an intermediate height between $Y_I$ and $-R$. Thus, the equation for a partially drained spherical vessel is:

$$
t=\frac{2\pi R^2}{\sqrt{\dfrac{2gA_P^2}{K_P}}}
\left(\sqrt{C_{\Delta H}+R+Y_I}-\sqrt{C_{\Delta H}+R+Y_{Int}}\right)
$$

$$
-\frac{2\pi}{15\sqrt{\dfrac{2gA_P^2}{K_P}}}
\left\{
\frac{\left[8(C_{\Delta H}+R)^2-4(C_{\Delta H}+R)Y_I+3Y_I^2\right]}
{\sqrt{C_{\Delta H}+R+Y_I}}
-
\frac{\left[8(C_{\Delta H}+R)^2+4(C_{\Delta H}+R)Y_{Int}+3Y_{Int}^2\right]}
{\sqrt{C_{\Delta H}+R+Y_{Int}}}
\right\}.
$$
