## 20.4.1 New, Clean Steel Pipe

Here, we will calculate NPSHA at the suction entrance to the core spray pump to assure that it is greater than NPSHR during the postulated LOCA. The pump suction line portion of the core spray system is shown in Figure 20.3. Input parameters from Section 5.6.1 applicable to this evaluation, as well as vapor pressure of water at 120°F,* are listed below.

### 20.4.1.1 Input Parameters

All loss coefficients are in terms of velocity in 14" schedule 10 pipe.

$\rho_w = 61.7 \text{ lb/ft}^3$  
Weight density of water at 120°F during postulated LOCA.

$A_{14} = 0.9940 \text{ ft}^2$  
Flow area of 14" schedule 10 pipe.

$d_{14} = 13.500 \text{ in}$  
Inside diameter of 14" schedule 10 pipe.

$e = 0.001800 \text{ in}$  
Absolute roughness of new, clean steel pipe.

$Elev_1 = 18.0 \text{ ft}$  
Elevation of minimum water level in suppression chamber.

$Elev_2 = 0 \text{ ft}$  
Elevation of core spray pump suction inlet.

$f_{14} = 0.0134$  
Adjusted friction factor for flow in 14" schedule 10 pipe.

$g = 32.174 \text{ ft/s}^2$  
Acceleration of gravity.

$K_{Valve14} = 0.20$  
Loss coefficient of gate valve in pump suction line.

$K_{LREll14} = 0.177$  
Adjusted loss coefficient of 14" schedule 10, 90° long radius elbow.

$K_{Strainer} = 6.0$  
Loss coefficient of “dirty” strainer.

\* Over time, the water in the suppression chamber may heat up to 120°F during the LOCA event.

### 20.4.1.2 Solution

Use Equation 20.1 to calculate NPSHA as a function of core spray pump flow rate $\dot{w}_{Pump}$ (or vessel pressure) during the postulated LOCA:

$$
p_S = p_1 = 14.7 \text{ psia},
$$

$$
H_S = Elev_1 - Elev_2 = 18.0 - 0 = 18.0 \text{ ft},
$$

$$
K_L = K_{Strainer} + K_{Valve14} + 4K_{LREll14} + f_{14}\frac{L_{1,2}}{D_{14}}
$$

$$
= 6.0 + 0.20 + 4 \times 0.177 + 0.0134 \frac{40}{13.500/12}
$$

$$
= 7.38,
$$

$$
\text{NPSHA} = \frac{144 \times 14.7}{61.71} + 18.0 -
\frac{7.38}{2 \times 32.174 \times 61.71^2 \times 0.9940^2}\dot{w}_{Pump}^{\,2} -
\frac{144 \times 1.693}{61.71}
$$

$$
= 48.38 - 2.938 \times 10^{-5} \times \dot{w}_{Pump}^{\,2}.
$$

### 20.4.1.3 Results

As shown in Table 20.1, core spray pump flow rate as a function of vessel pressure during the postulated LOCA event was calculated in Section 5.6.1. From this, NPSHA was calculated as a function of pump flow rate.

Calculated NPSHA is compared to NPSHR data from the pump manufacturer in Figure 20.4. The NPSHA curves for the open and closed bypass condition overlap each other as they should. There is significant NPSH margin over the operating range of the core spray pump during the postulated LOCA event. NPSH margin is gained by closing the bypass valve because maximum pump flow is reduced by about 100 gpm.
