## 15.2 COILS

Coils of pipe or tubing can be classified as those with constant curvature, *helices*, and those with variable curvature, *spirals*. A helix is a three-dimensional coil that runs along the surface of a cylinder. A spiral is typically a planar curve (that is flat), like the grooves of a phonograph record or a DVD. Coils provide for a relatively large amount of surface area within a confined space, as in a heat exchanger. Furthermore, heat transfer coefficients in coils are higher than in straight pipes.

The curvature of a coil is defined as the ratio of the radius $r$ of the circle into which the tubing is bent to the inside diameter $d$ of the pipe. The distance between the central lines of two consecutive turns is the pitch $p$. In general, a helix has a constant pitch. An Archimedean spiral has a constant pitch; other spirals do not.
