76 LOSS COEFFICIENTS

The loss coefficient always represents the number of velocity heads, $V^2/2g$, lost. The numerical value of any loss coefficient is intimately related to the inherent velocity in the associated pressure drop equation. In many cases the relationship is self-evident and the loss coefficient is simply labeled as $K$. In cases where there is a change in flow area a subscript is used to denote the relationship. For example, in the case of a contraction, $K_2$ indicates that the loss coefficient is related to the velocity at point 2, the downstream velocity. In the case of flow through tees, a subscript—two numbers separated by a comma—defines the flow path, and a sub-subscript defines the related velocity. For instance, $K_{1,2_2}$ indicates that pressure loss is from point 1 to point 2 and the loss coefficient is related to the velocity at point 2.
