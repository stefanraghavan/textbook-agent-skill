drop in a pipe section composed of $n$ different elements can be determined by:

$$
\sigma_{dp} = \frac{\sqrt{\sum_{i=1}^{n}(N_i \sigma_i K_i)^2}}{\sum_{i=1}^{n} N_i K_i},
$$

(7.2)

where $N_i$ is the number of like or similar elements that share a common loss coefficient $K_i$.* The percentage uncertainty $\sigma_{dp}$ can be based on one, two, or three standard deviation(s).

\* Treat surface friction as a single element unless there are size or surface roughness differences along the length of the pipe section.
