A network flow model of the ring sparger is developed in order to evaluate the uniformity of flow among the individual spray nozzles, to determine the pressure distribution within the header, and to determine the pressure at the inlet tee. As shown in Figure 5.2, only one arm is modeled because of flow symmetry about the inlet tee. The nodes are shown in Figure 5.2 as heavy dots and are numbered from 0 to 7.

## 5.5.1 Ground Rules and Assumptions

- The water temperature is 70°F.
- The total flow rate to the sparger is 6000 gpm.
- According to the manufacturer, the loss coefficient $K_{Noz}$ of the flow nozzles is 10 in terms of velocity head in 3" schedule 40 pipe.
- Node 0 is located at the inlet to the sparger. Nodes 1–6 are located within the sparger arm just upstream of the diverging branches (i.e., in the common channel). Node 7 is located inside the test bay outside the sparger and is at atmospheric pressure.
- Ignore coupling effects.
- Use Equation 16.18 to model pressure loss through the inlet tee. Assume that the radius ratio $r/d$ of the branch edge equals 0.20.
- Use Equation 16.7 to model pressure loss due to diverging flow through run at Nodes 1 through 6.
- Use Equation 16.15 to model pressure loss due to diverging flow through branch at Nodes 1 through 6. Assume that the branch edge is sharp (radius ratio $r/d$ equals zero).
